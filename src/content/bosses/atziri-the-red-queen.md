## Full Fight Guide

Atziri, the Red Queen is the pinnacle boss of Atziri's Temple (the Fate of the Vaal mechanic). Reaching her requires building the Temple up to at least Area Level 75, defeating Xipocado, the Royal Architect (who spawns on a random tile in your layout), and using his console to place the Royal Access Chamber. Path your temple layout to connect to that chamber, and Atziri's door unlocks.

There's no mid-fight resurrection mechanic here, if you fail, your entire path and unlocked rooms reset and you need to rebuild the route from scratch. Because of this, it's worth maximizing your level, gear, and Ascendancy points before attempting her rather than rushing in underprepared.

### Note on difficulty

Patch 0.5.4 lowered pinnacle boss resistances across the board, including Atziri's, alongside targeted nerfs to a few other pinnacle encounters. If you bounced off this fight in an earlier patch, it's meaningfully more farmable now.

## Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

### Phase 1 — Atziri, the Red Queen (100–10% Health)

Atziri enters the fight in her humanoid queen form. According to poe2db data, her Phase 1 form has 742% increased Life, 33% Energy Shield from Life, +33% Armour, +33% Evasion, and 30/30/30/0 elemental resistances. She is a Level 68 boss with a 380% damage multiplier and a 5% critical strike chance with +30% critical damage bonus. Her attack range is 12–22 units, significantly longer than most bosses, so do not assume you are safe at what feels like mid-range distance.

Her melee attacks randomly convert 40% of Physical damage to either Fire or Lightning. You can read which element is coming by watching the horns on her headdress—they light up to telegraph the damage type of her next attack. This visual cue is critical for reactive flask usage and positioning.

#### Key Mechanics in Phase 1

**Blood Spear**: Atziri hurls a spear that crashes down at your location and explodes into a nova of approximately 50 projectiles. These projectiles apply Corrupted Blood (stacking physical damage-over-time) and Maim (reduced movement speed). The spear has a 22-second cooldown, so you have time between uses, but the nova is devastating if you are caught near the impact point. Dodge away from the landing marker and do not try to facetank the projectile spray—getting hit by multiple projectiles stacks Corrupted Blood rapidly.

**Spinning Flame Wall**: Atziri swings her spear in a wide circle, leaving a ring of burning ground. In Phase 1, this ring is contained and avoidable by stepping inside or outside it. Do not stand in the lingering fire; the ignite stacks can eat through your life pool quickly if you are already debuffed from Blood Spear.

**Flameblast**: Atziri channels and then detonates a large marked zone. The channel is visible as a glowing area on the ground—leave it immediately. The detonation deals 1148–1722 Fire damage at Level 68 with a 6% critical strike chance and a 50% "more damage at centre" modifier, meaning a direct hit in the center of the blast is significantly worse than a glancing blow at the edge. The cooldown is 7 seconds for the standard version and 25 seconds for the empowered version.

**Storm Call**: Rows of lightning runes appear on the ground, and after a short delay, they explode in sequence. The narrow safe line runs down the center between the rune rows—position yourself there as soon as you see the runes forming. The single Storm Call has a 5-second cooldown, while the empowered version has a 25-second cooldown and covers a much larger area.

### Phase 2 — Atziri, the Fell Serpent (Below 10% Health in Phase 1)

When Atziri reaches approximately 10% health in Phase 1, she escapes through a mirror portal to a new arena and transforms into the Fell Serpent, restoring her health to full. This is not a quick transition—you are in a completely new arena with new terrain.

All of her Phase 1 attacks return in empowered versions:

- **Empowered Spinning Flame Wall**: The burning ring now covers the entire outer portion of the arena in lingering ignited ground, forcing you to fight in close quarters near the center. Attempting to kite around the outer ring is suicide—there is simply no safe ground there. This is the single biggest adjustment from Phase 1: you must fight close, not far.
- **Blood Spear Barrage**: Instead of a single spear, she fires four consecutive Blood Spears in rapid succession. Each one creates a 50-projectile nova. Surviving this requires sustained mobility and pre-positioning—do not stand still between spears.
- **Empowered Storm Call**: The rune rows are wider and more numerous, but the center safe line still exists. Memorize its position as your default fallback—any time you see runes forming, head for the center gap immediately.
- **Teleport Slam**: Atziri teleports to your location and slams down, dealing heavy Physical and Fire damage. The teleport has a short windup; if you see her vanish, start moving. Dodge rolling on reaction is possible but tight.

### Spreading Corruption (Below ~20% Health in Phase 2)

At approximately 20% health in Phase 2, Atziri coils into a ball and a lethal, expanding red corruption zone spreads outward from her position toward the arena edges. This is her enrage mechanic. You have two options: burst her down immediately from a safe distance before the zone covers the arena, or retreat to the small safe zone at the arena's far edge and wait for the attack to end. The safe zone is small and not obvious—knowing where it is before you reach this phase is critical. If you are caught in the corruption zone without a plan, you will die.

## Preparation & Gear Checklist

- **Fire Resistance capped at 75%**: This is non-negotiable. The majority of Atziri's damage across both phases is Fire-based—Flameblast, Spinning Flame Wall, and her converted melee attacks all deal Fire damage. If your Fire resistance is below cap, even non-critical hits will chunk large portions of your life.
- **Lightning Resistance capped at 75%**: Her Storm Call and converted melee attacks deal Lightning damage. Both elements must be capped.
- **Chaos Resistance**: Atziri has 0% Chaos Resistance according to poe2db, meaning Chaos builds deal full damage, but she also applies Corrupted Blood (Physical DoT) rather than Chaos. Still, decent Chaos Resistance helps with any incidental Chaos from the Temple rooms leading up to her.
- **Level 75+**: The Temple must be Area Level 75 to even access her. Your character should match or exceed that.
- **Anti-Bleed / Corrupted Blood counter**: A flask with "Bleeding removed" or a Staunching charm is highly recommended. Getting hit by Blood Spear's nova without a way to clear Corrupted Blood stacks means you will rapidly bleed out.
- **Movement skill**: Essential for dodging Flameblast zones, navigating Storm Call safe lines, and repositioning during Blood Spear Barrage.
- **Sustained recovery**: Life leech, regeneration, or a life flask that auto-recharges. The Phase 2 fight is long because she resets to full health, and you will need sustained recovery to outlast her sustained damage.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trying to kite at range in Phase 2**: The empowered Spinning Flame Wall covers the entire outer arena in fire. Kiting at range means standing in ignited ground. Close-quarters fighting near the center is the only viable strategy.
2. **Not knowing the Storm Call safe line**: The center gap between rune rows is your default safe position. If you do not know it exists, Storm Call will eventually catch you in an inescapable pattern.
3. **Ignoring the headdress horn cues**: Atziri's horns telegraph whether her next attack converts to Fire or Lightning. This information tells you which resistance is about to be tested. If you see Fire horns and your Fire Resistance is low, pop a flask or reposition defensively.
4. **Attempting the fight under-leveled or under-geared**: Because there is no resurrection mechanic, dying means rebuilding the entire Temple path from scratch. This is a significant time investment. Do not attempt Atziri until your character is genuinely ready.
5. **Getting caught by multiple Blood Spear projectiles**: The nova fires roughly 50 projectiles. Getting hit by one is manageable; getting hit by ten at once stacks Corrupted Blood to lethal levels. Dodge the impact point, not individual projectiles.

## Loot Table & Rewards

Atziri's drop pool is one of the most valuable among pinnacle bosses. According to poe2db, she has +800% item quantity, +3200% item rarity, +4800% item rarity (tier 3 boss modifier), a 25% chance to drop a map key of at least the same tier, and +9000% experience. She guarantees at least one rare item per kill.

**Atziri's Medallion** is the key drop, unlocking Atziri's Vault and six Royal Troves. These troves are the source of several high-value items:
- **Atziri's Rule** (staff): A powerful endgame staff for spellcasters.
- **Architect's Orb**: A currency item used in the Temple mechanic.
- **Orb of Sacrifice** (new in 0.5.4): A new currency item introduced alongside the Fate of the Vaal rework.

Other notable uniques from her pool include:
- **Drillneck** (quiver): A classic physical-damage quiver that remains relevant for arrow builds.
- **Atziri's Contempt** (spear): A Vaal-themed spear with defensive modifiers.
- **Atziri's Step** (boots): Evasion boots with spell suppression and life.
- **Sacrificial Regalia** (body armour): An Energy Shield chest with Vaal-themed modifiers.

## Tips for Specific Builds

### Melee / Close-Range Builds
Phase 2 is actually where melee builds shine, because the empowered Flame Wall forces close-quarters play on everyone. You are already in your comfort zone while ranged builds have to adjust. Watch for the Teleport Slam—dodge roll on reaction when she vanishes. Your biggest threat is Blood Spear Barrage at close range; the nova radius is wide enough that you need to create distance before impact, then close again after the projectiles pass.

### Ranged / Projectile Builds
Phase 1 is comfortable for you, but Phase 2 demands a complete positional reset. You cannot kite at range anymore. Stand near the center, use your projectiles to maintain damage while staying mobile, and treat the Flame Wall's fire ring as an absolute boundary. Skills that pierce or chain are valuable because Atziri moves aggressively in serpent form and you may not always have a clear line of sight.

### Spellcaster / Caster Builds
Atziri's 30/30/30 elemental resistances mean you should avoid relying on a single element if possible. Cold and Chaos spells bypass her Fire and Lightning resistance considerations. Chaos damage in particular is effective since she has 0% Chaos Resistance. Flameblast is your biggest threat—do not try to out-damage the channel; just leave the zone. If you are running a Fire build, be aware that her Fire Resistance is 30%, which is not insurmountable but does reduce your effective damage compared to Cold or Chaos alternatives.
