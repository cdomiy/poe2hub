## Full Fight Guide

Tavakai, the Consumed is the true final boss of Act 4, found in the Heart of the Tribe after sailing to Ngakanu. He's a three-phase escalation (Chieftain, the Fallen, the Consumed) dealing Physical, Cold, Fire, and Lightning damage across the fight, with Karui ancestor totems adding randomized god-power hazards throughout Phases 2 and 3.

This fight is widely described as more of an endurance and positioning check than a raw damage race, none of his individual attacks are overwhelming, but the persistent, overlapping ground hazards will drain your flasks fast if you don't actively clear space rather than tanking through them.

### Preparation

Cap Physical and Cold resistance at minimum, ideally rounding out Fire and Lightning too given his full elemental spread. Flasks that automatically generate charges are close to mandatory here, without them, most players run dry well before the fight ends. A mix of sustain (life regeneration, leech, or recharge) and a genuine repositioning skill will save you from the overlapping ground effects far more reliably than raw tankiness alone.

Before the fight, explore the Heart of the Tribe area to find the **Meeting House Secret**—a room outlined with chests full of loot. This is your last opportunity for gear upgrades before the Tavakai encounter, and it is worth taking the time to open.

## Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

### Phase 1 — Tavakai, the Chieftain (100–50% Health)

The fight begins in the Heart of the Tribe. Phase 1 is the easiest phase, and your goal is to use as few flask charges as possible while building a rhythm for dodging his telegraphed attacks.

**Axe and Spear Swings**: Wide, Physical-damage melee attacks. They are slow and clearly telegraphed. Tavakai moves slowly in this phase, so dodge rolling to the side and counterattacking is straightforward.

**Vaulting Slam Combos**: Tavakai leaps into the air and slams down, creating a shockwave. The telegraph is clear—he pauses briefly before leaping. Dodge diagonally or directly away. Burning ground patches appear where he lands, dealing stacking Fire damage over time. Do not stand in them.

**Kunai-on-a-Rope Pull**: Tavakai throws a kunai on a rope that pulls you toward him. If you get pulled in, follow up attacks can chain. Use a movement skill to break away immediately if caught.

**Jade Crystal Explosions**: Tavakai creates Jade Crystals that explode after a delay. Stay away from them—the explosion radius is generous enough that simply walking away works. The explosion also provides a counterattack window while he recovers.

**Ground Spike Eruptions**: Physical and Cold damage spikes erupt from the ground in telegraphed patterns. These establish the template for the rest of the fight: wide, lingering telegraphed zones that punish stationary play.

At 50% health, Tavakai stops attacking and transforms. A shockwave hits you, knocking you to the ground. This officially begins Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — Tavakai, the Fallen (50–25% Health)

Tavakai moves to a second arena where you will find Makoru at the Altars. He regenerates his health to full upon entering this phase—a disheartening mechanic that means the fight is genuinely long. His attacks become faster and gain new effects.

**Rain of Jade Spikes**: Tavakai swings his axe skyward, and jade spikes rain down across the entire arena. Watch where they land—there is no safe zone, only gaps between impact points. This attack covers massive area.

**Jade Statues**: He summons three to five jade statues that animate after a delay and chase you, exploding on proximity for area damage. Kill them before they reach you or kite them into a cluster and dodge the simultaneous detonation.

**Blood Pool ("Lend me your hunger, Father of Chaos!")**: A pool of blood appears on the ground, and Tavakai stomps on it for area damage. Move away from the blood pool on the voice cue.

**Bloody Tentacles ("Weakness becomes you!")**: Tentacles sprout from his hands. If they hit you, they inflict **Shock, Ignite, and a Fire Resistance debuff** simultaneously. This is one of the most dangerous attacks in Phase 2 because the triple debuff amplifies all subsequent damage. Dodge roll behind him on the voice cue.

**Jade Spike Waves ("Unleash yourself, Valako!")**: Tavakai slams his bag into the ground, creating several waves of jade spikes that fly upward. The best way to avoid this is to **stay close to Tavakai**—the waves spread outward from his position, so the center near him is relatively safe while the edges of the arena get covered.

**Daughter of the Moon**: A massive beam or icicle strikes the ground for several seconds. Getting hit has a very high chance of death. This is Tavakai's hardest-hitting single attack in Phase 2.

#### Karui God Totems

Throughout Phase 2 and Phase 3, totems periodically invoke Karui god powers. These are randomized and add environmental hazards:

- **Rongokurai**: Summons chilling serpentine dragons that inflict Cold damage and Chill.
- **Tawhoa**: Covers the arena in vines that **reduce your damage while you stand in them**—actively avoid this debuff, as it makes the already-long fight even longer.
- **Ramako**: Drops an expanding fire orb that detonates, leaving burning ground.
- Other totems add cold beams or lightning strikes.

The individual hazards rarely hit hard, but they add up fast. They are a resource drain more than a burst-damage check. Position away from totems invoking debuffs you do not want, particularly Tawhoa's vines and anything that heals Tavakai.

### Phase 3 — Tavakai, the Consumed (25–0% Health)

At approximately 25% health, Tavakai transforms into his final form and **fully heals again**. This is the hardest phase, as his attacks are buffed and every dodge-required attack now randomly invokes one of the Karui god powers alongside it, stacking mechanics unpredictably.

**Weapon Combo + Leap Slam Series**: Tavakai chains a weapon combo ending in a series of Leap Slams. Dodge these attacks and avoid counterattacking—the recovery windows are shorter than in previous phases.

**Shadow Dragons from Rifts**: Rifts open in the ground, and Shadow Dragons spawn from them. The rifts contain visible stars, confirming they are dimensional portals. These dragons inflict debuffs—stay away from them.

**Tawhoa's Forest**: Tavakai covers the entire arena with greenery. Standing in it triggers the **Tawhoa's Forest debuff**, which reduces all damage you deal. The only safe zone is in the center of the map, but Tavakai is likely there. Navigate carefully and prioritize reaching the center over dealing damage.

**Exploding Blood Crystals**: Blood crystals erupt from the ground and explode. Avoid standing on or near them—these are telegraphed but can be hard to spot in the visual chaos of Phase 3.

**Small Sun**: Tavakai summons a small sun that rises and then explodes. The explosion leaves behind a smaller sun and burning ground. Once the sun hits the ground, it deals massive area damage. This is one of the deadliest attacks in Phase 3—get far away from the sun when it appears.

**Erupting Volcano**: A volcano spews flames onto the ground, adding persistent Fire hazards to an already cluttered arena.

**Chasing Lightning Bolts** (late Phase 3): Lightning bolts that chase the player, applying Shock and slowing you down. Being Shocked makes all of Tavakai's other attacks more dangerous. A source of Shock removal is essential here.

The key insight for Phase 3 is that **Tavakai's attacks stay slow and telegraphed even in this phase**. Every individual attack is dodgeable. The problem is the stacking—ground hazards from god powers, blood crystals, burning ground, and greenery all compress your available space simultaneously. Patience punishes this fight less than panic does. Survive, wait for an opening, deal one burst of damage, then retreat and reset.

## Preparation & Gear Checklist

- **Physical Resistance capped**: His core damage type across all phases.
- **Cold Resistance capped**: Ground spikes, Rongokurai's dragons, and Daughter of the Moon all deal Cold damage.
- **Fire Resistance capped**: Burning ground, Ramako's fire orb, the Small Sun, and the erupting volcano all deal Fire damage.
- **Lightning Resistance**: Chasing lightning bolts in Phase 3 and various god-power lightning strikes. Not as dominant as the other three, but still present.
- **Level 56+**: The Heart of the Tribe is a high-level Act 4 zone. Being under-leveled makes his already-large health pool even harder to chew through.
- **Auto-recharging Life and Mana Flasks**: Close to mandatory. The fight can easily last 5–8 minutes. Without flask charge generation, you will run dry in Phase 3.
- **Shock Removal**: A flask with "Shock removed" or a charm that provides Shock immunity. The chasing lightning bolts in late Phase 3 make this essential.
- **Movement Skill**: Flame Dash, Frostblink, or Leap Slam for repositioning out of overlapping ground hazards. You will need it repeatedly.
- **Anti-Chill / Freeze**: Cold damage is prevalent. A source of Chill/Freeze removal prevents you from getting pinned in place during Phase 2's god-power sequences.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Front-loading flask charges in Phase 1**: Phase 1 is the easiest part of the fight. If you are burning flask charges here, you will not have enough for Phase 2's persistent hazards and Phase 3's stacking mechanics. Pace yourself.
2. **Tanking through Tawhoa's vine debuff**: The damage reduction from standing in the vines makes the fight dramatically longer. Actively avoid the green zones even if it means losing a damage window.
3. **Trying to force damage in Phase 3**: The correct play is to survive first, then deal damage in the brief openings between attack patterns. Trying to burst through Phase 3 gets you killed by stacking ground effects.
4. **Ignoring the Small Sun**: The Small Sun's explosion deals massive area damage. Running away from it is always the correct response, even if it means giving up a damage window.
5. **Getting Shocked by lightning bolts without a removal plan**: Shock amplifies all damage. Without a way to clear it, the chasing lightning bolts in late Phase 3 will make every subsequent attack potentially lethal.

## Loot Table & Rewards

Tavakai does not drop unique items. His loot pool contains rare and magic items that are valuable for early Act 4 progression—these can be used as direct upgrades or converted into currency through vendor recipes. The primary reward is story completion: defeating Tavakai finishes The Search questline and officially concludes Act 4, unlocking the Interludes and the next chapter of your journey.

After defeating Tavakai, The Hooded One appears and cures both Tavakai and Makoru. You then speak with Tavakai, return to Kingsmarch, and speak with The Hooded One to proceed.

## Tips for Specific Builds

### Melee / Close-Range Builds
Phase 1 is comfortable for you—dodge the vaulting slams and counterattack during recovery. Phase 2 is where melee struggles, because the jade statues, blood pools, and tentacle attacks all punish close proximity. The exception is the Jade Spike Waves attack ("Unleash yourself, Valako!"), where staying close to Tavakai is actually the safest position. In Phase 3, you must play hit-and-run: get one clean combo in after a major attack, then retreat. Do not stay close for more than a few seconds at a time.

### Ranged / Projectile Builds
You have an easier time avoiding the close-range tentacle attacks and jade statue detonations. However, the Rain of Jade Spikes covers the entire arena, so you cannot simply stand at range and tank through it. Keep moving, prioritize dodging over dealing damage, and use skills that allow you to attack while mobile (thrown weapons, projectile spells, totems). In Phase 3, your priority is the same as everyone else's: survive the stacking hazards and deal damage in openings.

### Spellcaster / Caster Builds
Cold and Chaos spells are effective since Tavakai does not have exceptional resistance to either. Fire spells work against his non-Fire-resistant phases but compete with the burning ground hazards for visual clarity—adding more fire effects to an already cluttered arena can make it harder to see enemy attacks. Cold spells that Chill or Freeze are particularly valuable for creating extra reaction windows during Phase 2's god-power sequences. Totems and minions can serve as decoys for the Shadow Dragons and chasing lightning bolts.
