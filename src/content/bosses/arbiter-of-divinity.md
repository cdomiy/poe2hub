## Full Fight Guide

The Arbiter of Divinity replaced the Arbiter of Ash as PoE2's top pinnacle boss in the 0.5 Return of the Ancients update. It lives in the Origin Tower at the center of the Precursor Fortress, and you can't reach it cold — you need to defeat the Arbiter of Ash first, then assemble an **Origin Core** from two keys dropped by bosses in two new wings of the Fortress. Your first encounter happens automatically through the Origins of Divinity questline at reduced difficulty. Every subsequent fight is the full pinnacle version, unlocked through the Atlas key system.

The unlock chain is genuinely confusing for many players, so here is the full sequence. First, reach the Precursor Fortress by completing your first Precursor (Lost) Tower in the endgame Atlas. Push the Fortress route inward toward the Burning Monolith, collecting your three Crisis Fragments along the way. Insert the three Crisis Fragments at the Burning Monolith and kill the Arbiter of Ash — this is the gate that opens the next part of the Fortress and reveals two new wings around the Origin Tower. Next, clear **Patriarch Hall** (boss **Phya** drops the **Origin Spark**) and **Matriarch Hall** (boss **Phyx** drops the **Origin Cradle**). Both require Tier 15 Waystones and have **limited attempts** — failing a boss inside costs you the run and forces you to re-farm Tier 15 maps to reset. Take both keys to the Origin Tower and slot the Origin Spark and Origin Cradle into the **Precursor Reactor** to forge the **Origin Core**. Place the Origin Core in the socket at the top of the tower, climb the stairs, and the fight begins.

The limited-attempts rule on the halls is the real friction. Bank a couple of Origin Sparks and Cradles before your first Arbiter attempt so a single death doesn't send you all the way back through the entire key chain.

## Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

### Phase 1 — Divine Assault (100% to ~40% HP)

The Arbiter of Divinity is a **spear-wielding duelist** — fast, melee-heavy, with sweeping golden AoE. Phase 1 is about learning his moveset, which is heavily telegraphed but punishing if you greed. Here is every attack and how to handle it:

**Dashing Spear Thrust.** The Arbiter dashes to your position and thrusts his spear. Side-roll **through** the dash, not away from it — dodging away keeps you in the thrust's path longer. This is his most frequent gap-closer, so recognizing the wind-up animation is essential.

**Spear / Double Spear Thrust.** A close-range one- or two-hit thrust combo. Stay mobile and never stand directly in front of him for more than a moment. The double thrust has a slight pause between hits — don't dodge-roll the first and then stand still for the second.

**Upper Spear Slash.** An upward slash for heavy damage. Roll out at the wind-up animation (a distinctive upward pull of the spear) and punish after the whiff. The recovery window is generous if you dodge the initial slash.

**Golden Charged Lunge.** A mid-range charging lunge with a golden glow. Dodge perpendicular to the charge line. The telegraph is a brief pause as he coils before launching, giving you a clear cue to dodge sideways.

**AoE Divine Slam.** The Arbiter leaps into the air and crashes down, creating a large area-of-effect zone. Get out of the marked circle on the ground before he lands — the impact zone is clearly telegraphed by a glowing circle. This is one of his highest-damage moves and the telegraph is your friend.

**Heavy Combo.** A two-part attack: ground slam followed by a spinning slash. You need two separate dodge rolls — one for the slam, one for the spin. Players who roll the slam and assume the combo is over eat the spinning slash.

**Ranged Combo.** Fires two slashing energy projectiles in sequence, ending in a beam. Strafe the projectiles laterally and respect the beam line — the beam is the real threat, tracking your position briefly before locking in. Do not panic-dodge backward; lateral movement is safer.

**Divine Electrical Orbs.** Releases slow-moving orbs of electricity that deal **lightning damage**. Weave between the orbs rather than trying to outrun them. These are the primary reason to prioritize lightning mitigation in your gear setup.

**Aerial Assault.** An aerial slam combined with ranged slashes. Reposition under him as he lands — the impact zone is at his landing point, so being underneath during the aerial phase is counterintuitively safe, then reposition away from the subsequent slashes.

### Phase 2 — Aspects of Divinity (~40% HP)

At roughly 40% health, the Arbiter becomes **much faster and more aggressive**. He keeps the entire Phase 1 kit and adds new attacks on top:

**Extended Melee Combo.** A longer melee string that baits you into dodging early and then punishes. Let him commit to the full string before you reposition — do not try to trade during this combo.

**Super Dash Thrust.** A long-range gap-closer that covers massive distance. Pre-roll the moment you see the telegraph — this comes out fast and one-shots undergeared characters.

**Triple Spinning Slash.** Three rapid spins that cover a wide area around him. Create distance immediately and do not try to trade — the hitbox is deceptively large on each spin.

**Rain of Divine Lightning.** Heavy ranged **lightning** AoE that covers falling zones across the arena. Keep moving constantly and do not stand in any marked area. This is where capped lightning resistance pays off.

**The Aspects of Divinity — the mechanic that wipes people.** In Phase 2, the Arbiter spawns **mirror images of himself** called the Aspects of Divinity. These clones can mirror many of the boss's attacks and create additional threats coming from multiple directions. If you focus only on the original Arbiter, you will be overwhelmed by crossfire. The counter is built into the arena: **collect the Divine Power orbs** located on circular platforms at the arena's edge. Grabbing an orb grants **temporary immunity**, and that window is your opportunity to destroy the Aspects before re-engaging the boss. Players who ignore the orbs and try to out-DPS the clones directly are the most common Phase 2 deaths.

**Arena-Wide Circle Attack.** At certain points in Phase 2, numerous circles appear across the entire arena simultaneously, covering almost the entire battlefield. There are small safe gaps between the danger zones — identify these openings immediately and move into them. Panic leads to running into circles; calm scanning leads to survival.

## Preparation & Gear Checklist

**Cap all elemental resistances at 75%.** Standard pinnacle prep, non-negotiable. The Arbiter deals primarily Fire and Lightning damage, and is resistant to all elements plus extremely resistant to Physical damage. Leaning on armour alone will not save you here.

**Prioritize Lightning mitigation.** Between the Divine Electrical Orbs and the Rain of Divine Lightning, a substantial chunk of his damage is lightning. Extra maximum lightning resistance or an Exposure-clearing layer pays significant dividends. Consider topaz flasks and lightning-resistant gear modding specifically for this encounter.

**Bring a real Life/ES pool.** This is not a fight you out-heal with a half-finished character. The two-phase structure plus limited-attempt entry means every death has a real cost in time and keys.

**Movement is your primary defense.** Almost everything the Arbiter does is dodgeable with a reliable dodge-roll and a movement skill. Prioritize a fast, responsive movement skill (Flame Dash, Shield Charge) for repositioning during Phase 2's chaotic moments.

**Difficulty slider awareness.** Like other 0.5 pinnacle invitations, this fight has a difficulty slider (0–4) that trades respawn count for better rewards: five respawns at Difficulty 0 down to a single attempt at Difficulty 4. If your Arbiter of Ash kill needed multiple respawns, don't jump straight to higher Divinity difficulties — the Phase 2 clone sequence gets meaningfully less forgiving as your respawn budget shrinks.

**Bank extra keys.** Each kill consumes a full Origin Core (and therefore a set of T15-gated keys from Patriarch and Matriarch Halls). The boss has a real entry cost, which is exactly why a clean, fast clear matters and why under-geared attempts are so expensive. Stockpile a few sets of Origin Sparks and Cradles before your first few attempts.

## Common Mistakes

**1. Overcommitting damage in Phase 1.** The Arbiter's spear combos are heavily telegraphed but punishing. Players who try to squeeze in one more attack during a combo window eat a Double Spear Thrust or an Upper Spear Slash that costs them half their health bar. Respect the spacing — wait for the full combo to finish, then punish.

**2. Failing to recognize the Phase 2 transition.** The moment Phase 2 triggers, the Arbiter briefly goes invincible and speeds up. Players who try to out-DPS the transition get caught by the clone beam sequence before they can reposition. The correct play is to immediately reposition toward the arena edge the instant you see the Phase 2 activation, then assess the situation.

**3. Ignoring the Divine Power orbs.** This is the single biggest Phase 2 killer. The orbs are not optional — they are the designed counter to the Aspects of Divinity. Trying to tank or out-DPS the clones without immunity gets you overwhelmed by crossfire from multiple directions. Grab an orb, pop immunity, clear the Aspects, then return to the boss.

**4. Panicking during the arena-wide circle attack.** When circles cover the entire arena, panic leads to running into danger zones. Calmly scan for the small safe gaps between circles and move into them. The gaps exist by design — the attack is a positioning check, not a death sentence.

## Loot Table & Rewards

The Arbiter of Divinity has one of the most rewarding loot pools in PoE2's 0.5 endgame. The fight drops **four exclusive Unique items**, all of which are restricted to this boss and cannot be chanced:

**Decree of Loyalty (Ancient Mail, Body Armour).** The chase unique. Requires Level 70, 67 Str, 67 Int. Provides (200–300)% increased Armour and Energy Shield, +(15–25) Strength and Intelligence, +(1–5)% to Maximum Chaos Resistance, and uniquely converts 1% of maximum Life to twice as much Armour per 1% Chaos Resistance above 0%. It also lets you Defend with (150–200)% of Armour while you have Energy Shield, and prevents Damage over Time from bypassing Energy Shield. This item defines entire builds around the Chaos Resistance-to-Armour conversion mechanic.

**Decree of Acuity (Ancient Visor, Helmet).** Requires Level 65, 59 Str, 59 Dex. Provides (150–250)% increased Armour and Evasion, +(25–35) Dexterity, and Gain (15–30)% of Evasion Rating as extra Armour. The drawback: You are Blind, but the Effect of Blind on you is reversed. A powerful helmet for Armour/Evasion hybrid builds.

**Decree of Flight (Ancient Leggings, Boots).** Requires Level 65, 56 Str, 56 Int. Provides 30% increased Movement Speed, (150–200)% increased Armour and Energy Shield, (20–30)% faster Dodge Roll, and Gain Guard equal to (10–20)% of missing Energy Shield for 4 seconds when you Dodge Roll. The Maximum Guard amount is based on maximum Energy Shield. Exceptional for dodge-roll-centric ES builds.

**The Ordained (Grand Spear, Weapon).** Requires Level 79, 68 Str, 109 Dex. Provides (150–250)% increased Physical Damage, Adds 1 to (200–300) Lightning Damage, +(5–8)% to Critical Hit Chance, and Life Leech recovers based on Lightning damage as well as Physical. Grants Level 18 Righteous Descent and creates a Fragment of Divinity in your Presence every 4 seconds. A build-defining weapon for spear builds.

Beyond these exclusive drops, each Arbiter of Divinity kill also awards **Atlas Tree points** for completing the Fortress/Origins of Divinity content. Five total kills complete the Fortress section of the Atlas Tree, making repeated farming valuable for both loot and progression.

## Tips for Specific Builds

**Melee / Spear Builds.** You have the advantage of understanding the Arbiter's weapon range intimately. Your own spear reach means you can punish his whiffed thrusts from just outside his range without overcommitting. The Ordained Grand Spear is the natural chase item for your build. During Phase 2, be especially careful about the Super Dash Thrust — as a melee character, you are always in its effective range, so pre-rolling when you see the telegraph is essential.

**Ranged / Casting Builds.** Maintain maximum distance during Phase 1 and respect the Ranged Combo's beam line — it tracks your position briefly before locking in. During Phase 2, the Rain of Divine Lightning is your biggest threat because the falling zones can cover your preferred kiting paths. Prioritize lightning resistance and keep moving. The Divine Power orbs are located at the arena's edge, which is naturally where ranged builds position, so you should have easy access to them.

**Tank / High-EHP Builds.** The Arbiter's damage profile (Fire and Lightning with some Physical) rewards builds that can survive a clipped hit and keep fighting. Decree of Loyalty is the ultimate chase item for tank builds — the Chaos Resistance-to-Armour conversion scales exponentially with investment. During Phase 2, you have more margin to survive the Aspects' attacks while collecting Divine Power orbs, but do not overestimate your tankiness against the arena-wide circle attack — it hits through most defensive layers.

**High-Mobility / Dodge Builds.** This fight rewards your archetype more than almost any other pinnacle encounter. Almost every attack is dodgeable, and the Decree of Flight boots (with their faster Dodge Roll and Guard-on-Dodge-Roll mechanic) are practically designed for you. During Phase 2, your mobility makes orb collection and Aspect clearing efficient — use it to control the pace of the fight rather than frantically racing.
