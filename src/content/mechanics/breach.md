## Core loop

A Breach opens a dimensional rift in the map that floods the area with Hiveborn enemies. The
encounter runs on a dual-bar timer: a dark bar that always decays toward zero, and a light bar
that fills as you kill enemies. Kill fast enough that the light bar outpaces the dark bar's
decay, and the combined progress hits 100% — that triggers a **Stabilised Breach**, which spawns
a tougher wave of elite enemies followed by the miniboss Vruun, Marshal of Xesht. Defeating Vruun
collapses the portal and pays out the full reward: Hiveblood, Breach Splinters, and a chance at
a Wombgift.

## Progression path

- Collect Breach Splinters from regular map encounters and combine them into a **Breachstone**.
- Use the Breachstone to reveal a **Breach Domain** on the Atlas — a dedicated cluster of maps
  containing Hive and Fortress content.
- **Hive**: a walled region around a breach. Break through the walls to reach the inner area,
  then summon Ailith and defend her while she destroys the Hive from within.
- **Fortress**: a larger-scale Hive with progressively harder enemy waves, leading to the
  Breachlords **Tul and Esh** as its final bosses.
- Beyond the Breachlords lies the true pinnacle of the mechanic: **Xesht**, reached through the
  High Fortress and Twisted Domain areas.

## The Genesis Tree

Hiveblood collected from Breach encounters feeds the **Genesis Tree**, a crafting station at the
Monastery of the Keepers hub with four independent branches — Rings, Amulets, Belts, and
Currency. Each branch is configured separately (modifier type, minimum modifier level, base type
preference) and activated by hatching a Wombgift.

## Practical tips

- Prioritize movement and area coverage over pure single-target damage — Breach rewards builds
  that can keep killing while repositioning as enemies flood in from multiple directions.
- Running Breach-boosting Atlas tablets (particularly ones that add extra rare monsters on
  Stabilise) multiplies returns significantly once your Atlas passive investment is in place —
  the 0.5.1 fix specifically made this investment pay off correctly for the first time.
- If your build struggles with sustained single-target pressure on rares, the wave-timing at
  Vruun and beyond will be the harder part of the mechanic, not raw damage.
