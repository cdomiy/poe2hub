## Overview

Atziri's Temple is a modular, grid-based dungeon you build one run at a time, now fully integrated into Path of Exile 2's core endgame as of patch 0.5.0 Return of the Ancients. Unlike traditional mapping, the Temple rewards planning as much as combat skill — the rooms you choose, the paths you create, and the upgrades you prioritize all influence the value of your runs. The system is tied to the Fate of the Vaal mechanic and is accessed by collecting Energised Crystals from Vaal Beacons found throughout zones and maps. Once you have enough crystals, you open portals to the Temple and grind loot from its increasingly dangerous chambers. The Temple sits on the Atlas northeast of your starting location, in the city of Lira Vaal, and has its own dedicated Atlas Passive Tree with bonuses specific to the Temple itself. Temple Precursor Tablets can guarantee Vaal Beacons in your maps for players who want to focus on this mechanic. [(overgear.com)](https://overgear.com/guides/poe-2/temple-guide/) [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3931686)

## How It Works

**Accessing the Temple** begins with Vaal Beacons. You first find Vaal Beacons in Act 3 — activating six of them (by finishing encounters) unlocks the Temple in the present. Complete more Beacon encounters in the "Doryani's Contingency" Interlude to visit the Temple in the past (a separate timeline version). Once you reach the endgame, you can encounter Vaal Beacons in maps; the level of the Temple is based on the area level where the crystals were collected, so in order to open high-tier Temples, you need to run high-tier maps. The default Energised Crystal storage limit is 60 in patch 0.5.0, and opening a Temple requires 6 Energised Crystals. A new region on the Atlas with guaranteed Vaal Beacon encounters in every map provides points for the Temple Atlas Tree. [(overgear.com)](https://overgear.com/guides/poe-2/temple-guide/) [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3931686)

**Building the Temple** revolves around the Temple Console, where you manage three important resources: the Temple Grid (how rooms connect to one another), Room Cards (placed onto the grid to expand your layout), and Energised Crystals (the currency that lets you place rooms). Each visit offers a random selection of six room or corridor tiles; starting from one corner of the grid, you place tiles to extend a path toward the boss room on the far side, where Queen Atziri waits. Connections matter because certain upgrades require adjacent rooms. Rooms can now be upgraded to Tier 4 through the Fate of the Vaal Atlas Passive Tree, allowing more specialisation into particular room types. [(overgear.com)](https://overgear.com/guides/poe-2/temple-guide/)

**The Collapse Risk** is the Temple's central tension. Clearing a room, opening a strongbox, or defeating an Architect boss risks triggering a temple collapse, which can downgrade or outright destroy rooms in your layout. Without an Architect/Alva run, approximately 10% of rooms are removed; with an Architect run, approximately 30% are removed. Hotfix 0.5.1 tightened these to roughly 12% and 36% respectively. Rooms out in the open destabilize first; chained rooms with rooms after them are protected since destabilization only targets the start or end of paths. Straight chains preserve the middle. [(aoeah.com)](https://www.aoeah.com/news/4609--poe-2-05-vaal-temple-cheat-sheet-best-layouts--farm-guide)

**Medallions** drop from completed rooms and trigger powerful effects. You can hold up to six at a time. The main types include: Add a room (extra placement option), Reroll (rerolls your current room offers), Upgrade (increases a room's tier), and Lock (prevents a room from destabilizing — now rare, only from Atziri and the Architect). [(aoeah.com)](https://www.aoeah.com/news/4609--poe-2-05-vaal-temple-cheat-sheet-best-layouts--farm-guide)

**Bosses** — Two bosses live inside the Temple. **The Architect** is a recurring mid-temple boss encountered as you expand the layout. He drops the Lock medallion and gives you a console of restricted rooms — guaranteed-to-destabilize special rooms with strong rewards. The most important is the Royal Access Chamber, which is mandatory for fighting Atziri. The Architect has one deadly mechanic — a hard-to-see Frost Nova when you hug him, so bait it and then keep your distance. **Atziri, the Red Queen** is the Temple's pinnacle boss, found at the end of a fully built path. She is a two-phase fight: the first phase is the harder of the two, and in the second phase she turns into a snake and shoots lightning. Circle behind her to stay safe. To reach her, first kill the Architect, place the Royal Access Chamber, then on a separate run path to both the Royal Access Chamber and Atziri. [(aoeah.com)](https://www.aoeah.com/news/4609--poe-2-05-vaal-temple-cheat-sheet-best-layouts--farm-guide)

**Crafting Systems** — Fate of the Vaal introduces corrupted-item crafting distinct from the rest of the game: Ancient Soul Cores, Vaal-themed currencies, and four types of Vaal Infusers (Armourer's for Armour, Blacksmith's for Martial Weapons, Arcanist's for Wands/Staves/Sceptres, and Catalysing for Jewellery), all usable only on items at or above 20% Quality. Reward Rooms granted from the console after defeating the Architect provide powerful crafting outcomes.

## Strategy & Tips

- **Build toward a single serpentine path rather than spreading rooms across the grid.** This is not just efficient — it directly protects you from the collapse mechanic, since core rooms in a disjointed layout can end up unable to be safely bypassed. The off-path "Immortal Snake" layout is the most recommended strategy: draw a short main path, place the Royal Access Chamber exactly one tile off the main path, connect it with one disposable "buffer" room, and fill the rest with junk rooms at the far edges as collapse fodder. [(mmogah.com)](https://www.mmogah.com/news/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-05-the-atziri-vaal-temple-farming-guide)
- **Prioritize Smithy rooms early and upgrade them via Golem Works.** Higher-tier Smithies meaningfully increase the rarity of final reward chest loot.
- **Save the Architect for last in a run** since he is the deadlier of the two bosses. If you die to either boss, you can redo the fight, but the rest of the temple is wiped.
- **Since 0.5.0 made the Temple a permanent core mechanic**, older guides referencing the original Fate of the Vaal league numbers may describe a room-exploit strategy that no longer works after the anti-collapse patch. Rooms that are meant to be destabilized but cannot be removed without disconnecting parts of the Temple will instead be downgraded to paths. [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3931686)
- **The key rooms for a beginner farming Atziri** are the Royal Access Chamber (non-negotiable for Atziri access), Architect's Chamber (lets you manipulate the layout and sometimes place another Royal Access Chamber), and high-value crafting rooms like the Corruption Chamber, Alchemy Lab, and Sacrificial Chamber if they happen to sit on your path. [(mmogah.com)](https://www.mmogah.com/news/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-05-the-atziri-vaal-temple-farming-guide)
- **Run high-tier maps for Vaal Beacons** to ensure the Temple's level is as high as possible, directly improving the item level and value of drops inside.

## Rewards

The Temple's reward system is built around room types and their tier levels:

- **Reward Chests** — the primary loot source, scaling with room tier and type. Tier 4 rooms (unlocked via the Atlas Passive Tree) produce meaningfully better loot than lower tiers.
- **Vaal-themed Crafting Materials** — Ancient Soul Cores and Vaal Infusers enable corrupted-item crafting unavailable elsewhere in the game.
- **Atziri's Medallion** — dropped by Atziri upon defeat, taken back to the rewards center for high-value loot.
- **Corrupted Idols** — potential drops from high-tier rooms and Atziri herself, offering build-defining stats with downsides.
- **Vaal-themed Unique Items** — the Temple is the exclusive source for several Vaal-themed uniques that cannot drop from regular mapping content.
- **Lock Medallions** — rare drops from the Architect, preventing a room from destabilizing during collapse. [(aoeah.com)](https://www.aoeah.com/news/4609--poe-2-05-vaal-temple-cheat-sheet-best-layouts--farm-guide)
