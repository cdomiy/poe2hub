## Overview

Abyss in Path of Exile 2's 0.5 Return of the Ancients update is a fully reworked endgame mechanic that combines map-level density farming with a multi-layered pinnacle encounter and a unique sub-ascendancy system. The mechanic received its own introductory questline and a fixed Atlas location in patch 0.5.0, and the 0.5.3 reward pass transformed it from a relatively niche activity into one of the most consistently profitable farming strategies in the endgame. Abyss is a major source of Omens (particularly the Omen of Light and Omen of Abyssal Echoes), Abyss Jewels, and Desecrated Currency, and its pinnacle encounter — the Vessel of Kulemak — is the only source of the Grip of Kulemak ring and the only path to the Abyssal Lich sub-ascendancy. The mechanic scales from casual map encounters (just follow the fissures, kill the monsters, grab the loot) to high-investment Atlas-tree farming with stacked Abyss tablets for maximum density and profit. ## How It Works

**Map-Level Abyss** begins when killing monsters and opening chests in maps has a chance to reveal an Abyssal Depths entrance — a sub-level dungeon that ends in a mini-boss guaranteeing a chest or invitation, plus (as of 0.5.3) guaranteed Desecrated Currency. Fissures spread across the map, spawning monsters along their paths. Following the fissures to their end reveals the Depths entrance. The deeper you go, the more rares and Lichborn enemies you encounter, each providing increasingly valuable drops.

**Abyssal Depths** are the sub-level dungeons that spawn from map-level Abyss encounters. Clearing them leads to a mini-boss fight. Special versions of the Abyssal Depths are guarded by **Abyssal Commander bosses** — specifically Tasgul, Swallower of Light or Vandroth, Blackblooded Enslaver. These Commanders appear in level 79+ areas and are the source of Kulemak's Invitation, the key to the pinnacle encounter. As of 0.5.3, Abyssal Depths mini-bosses guarantee Desecrated Currency drops, making every Depths run profitable even if you do not get an Invitation. [(conquestcapped.com)](https://conquestcapped.com/guides/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-abyss-guide/)

**The Vessel of Kulemak (Pinnacle Encounter)** is Abyss's pinnacle fight, requiring Kulemak's Invitation. The Invitation drops with a 100% chance from either Tasgul or Vandroth in level 79+ Abyssal Depths, though neither boss is guaranteed to appear in a given map. You can force one of the two to show up by desecrating your Waystones for the "Abysses lead to an Abyssal Boss" modifier. The Invitation is a consumable and vanishes the moment you commit to the fight. Alternatively, the Invitation can be purchased on the trade site, trading for roughly 15 to 20 Exalted Orbs early in the league. [(conquestcapped.com)](https://conquestcapped.com/guides/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-abyss-guide/) [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-vessel-of-kulemak-guide)

The encounter itself is a multi-stage escalation fought in the Black Cathedral:

1. Use the Invitation at the Well of Souls and defeat the initial Vessel.
2. **Do not take the Finger reward yet.** Taking it early forfeits the bonus glove drop — this is the single most common mistake players make.
3. Interact with each of three petrified Liches (Kurgal, Ulaman, Amanamu) to revive the boss with an added Desecrated modifier each time. Every revival is harder than the last. Each Lich stamps a Desecrated modifier onto a brand-new Grip of Kulemak ring — up to 3 mods, one per faction.
4. After all three empowerments, return the Finger and fight the fully empowered Kulemak.
5. Defeating the full-strength version drops The Master's Reach (unique gloves) and an upgraded Grip of Kulemak ring. On a Lich character, the encounter also unlocks the **Abyssal Lich sub-ascendancy**. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-vessel-of-kulemak-guide) [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg/blog/poe-2/articles/poe2-witch-abyssal-lich-best-build)

**The Master's Reach** is a unique glove that reveals weaknesses on Rare and Unique enemies (functionally similar to the Huntress Ascendancy passive Predatory Instinct), and grants Untether, a skill that plants a seed of Abyssal Energy on an enemy that detonates for heavy physical damage on a follow-up hit or kill. Hitting a revealed weakness recoups 100% of damage taken from that enemy as life, plus bonus skill speed.

**The Abyssal Lich Sub-Ascendancy** is unlocked by completing the Vessel of Kulemak encounter and choosing "Return the Finger" after all three Lich empowerments. You play as a regular Lich until T14 maps, then unlock the upgrade. Once unlocked, you can freely toggle between regular Lich and Abyssal Lich. The boss retains its health between attempts — if you wipe, come back and finish the job. [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg/blog/poe-2/articles/poe2-witch-abyssal-lich-best-build)

## Strategy & Tips

- **Do not take the Finger reward before empowering all three Liches.** This is the single most common mistake that costs players the bonus glove drop (The Master's Reach). Interact with each Lich first, then return the Finger after all three empowerments.
- **Kulemak's Invitation went from a minor, low-value drop to one of the most valuable Abyss items** after the 0.5.3 reward pass. If you are farming Abyssal Depths, prioritize the mini-bosses that drop it. [(conquestcapped.com)](https://conquestcapped.com/guides/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-abyss-guide/)
- **Grip of Kulemak is a build-around ring** with a large, evolving modifier pool (Abyssal Wasting modifiers). It gained 13 new modifiers in 0.5.3. Check current values on the trade site rather than assuming older guides still reflect its stats, since it has been reworked multiple times. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-vessel-of-kulemak-guide)
- **Desecrate your Waystones** for the "Abysses lead to an Abyssal Boss" modifier to force one of the two Abyssal Commanders to appear, increasing your chances of getting Kulemak's Invitation.
- **For the Atlas tree, the recommended priority path** starts with Lightless Legions (always take this first), then Sprawling Rupture (spreads more fissures and spawns more monsters per Abyss), Unholy Influence (stacks extra Abyssal Modifiers onto monsters), and Dark Bloodlines (general boost). For Omen farming specifically, pair the sub-tree with every tablet-effect node you can path to. For raw rare density, layer in Adaptive Biology, No Simple Battles, and Mutating Monsters. [(conquestcapped.com)](https://conquestcapped.com/guides/path-of-exile-2/poe-2-abyss-guide/)
- **Use Hilda as your Atlas Master** for Abyss farming — she increases Lair Monsters and gives them more modifiers, which raises their Effectiveness and improves drops. Alternatively, use Doryani for the cleansed-map chance that can drop Fracturing Orbs.
- **The Abyssal Lich is locked behind T14+ farming.** You need to reach T14 maps and travel to the Well of Souls in the Mastodon Badlands (Act 2 waypoint after unlock) before you can use the Invitation. [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg/blog/poe-2/articles/poe2-witch-abyssal-lich-best-build)

## Rewards

Abyss offers several distinct reward categories:

- **Omens** — Abyss is a major Omen source in 0.5 (alongside Ritual). The headline drops are the Omen of Light and the Omen of Abyssal Echoes. Real farmers report roughly one Omen of Light every two maps with a tuned Amanamu setup, and targeted-craft Omens are some of the priciest consumables in the game. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-abyss-farming-guide)
- **Abyss Jewels** — socket into items with Abyssal Sockets. The big one is the Stygian Vise, a belt with a built-in Abyssal Socket; Liches in the Depths are a reliable source.
- **Heart of the Well (Diamond Jewels)** — drops from Lichborn enemies. Each one rolls four random Desecrated Modifiers, making it a chase item for endgame builds.
- **Grip of Kulemak** — the Abyssal Signet ring from the pinnacle encounter, a build-around item with a large, evolving modifier pool (13 new modifiers added in 0.5.3). [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-vessel-of-kulemak-guide)
- **The Master's Reach** — unique gloves from the fully empowered Kulemak fight, granting weakness revelation and the Untether skill.
- **Desecrated Currency** — guaranteed from Abyssal Depths mini-bosses as of 0.5.3.
- **Unique Abyss Items** — including Darkness Enthroned (a belt giving 50–100% increased effect of socketed jewels) and Undying Hate (a Timeless Jewel that rewrites passives into abyssal modifiers). [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-abyss-farming-guide)
- **Abyssal Lich Sub-Ascendancy** — unlocked through the pinnacle encounter, providing an alternative ascendancy for Lich characters with unique passive nodes.
