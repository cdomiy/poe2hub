## Overview

Expedition is one of the most consistent currency-farming mechanics in Path of Exile 2's 0.5 Return of the Ancients endgame. After its full rework in patch 0.5.0, the mechanic was deeply integrated into the Runes of Aldur league, transforming the old Logbook system into an ocean-exploring system anchored at the Ruins of Kingsmarch, a new hub area southeast of the Atlas starting location. When an Expedition spawns in a map, one of four NPCs appears in front of a burial site covered with Monster Markers, Chests, and Remnants. You receive a limited supply of explosives, lay a chain of charges across the markers you want, and detonate the whole line at once. The detonation unearths Runic Monsters — tougher rare enemies that drop Expedition artifacts and gear — while Remnants propagate modifiers that buff the monsters and loot from subsequent explosions in the chain. The result is a self-contained farming loop that scales from casual white-map runs all the way to high-density Grand Expeditions with multiple modifiers stacked on top of each other.

What makes Expedition stand out in 0.5 is its remarkably low barrier to entry. You do not need Tablets, you do not need expensive scarabs, and there is no dedicated Expedition Atlas passive tree — instead, generic Atlas nodes that increase Rare Monster quantity, Rare Monster rarity, and Monster Effectiveness carry over to Runic Monsters inside Expedition encounters, naturally improving your returns without requiring a specialized tree. The only real investment is rolling your maps to four or five modifiers so that Pack Size and Monster Effectiveness are high enough to make drops worthwhile, without making the content so deadly that you die and lose progress. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-expedition-farming-guide)

## How It Works

**Standard Expedition** is the everyday version that rolls naturally on maps, or can be forced in a region by slotting an Expedition Precursor Tablet into a completed Tower. When you encounter an Expedition, the NPC vendor standing at the site offers a detonation tool and a limited number of explosives (15 as of patch 0.5.3, reduced from the previous 20). You lay a fuse line by placing explosives so they form a chain that detonates sequentially; each blast unearths whatever sits inside its radius — Remnants, Runic Monsters, and chests. The critical rule governing Remnants is that a Remnant's modifiers only apply to Runic Monsters that spawn *after* that Remnant blows up. This means you should detonate reward-boosting Remnants first, then roll into the Runic Monster markers so that the "increased artifacts," "increased currency," and "increased pack size" modifiers all land on the packs you are about to fight. Each Remnant carries between three and ten runes — more runes mean more loot potential but also more waves and more danger. Clicking an un-detonated Remnant lets you preview and pick its outcome before committing. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-grand-expedition-farming-guide)

**Grand Expedition** is the high-ceiling successor to the old Logbook encounters. You open a Logbook by speaking to Dannig and choosing "show Expedition map," which generates a standalone area on the ocean. The zone is full of Remnants, Runic Monster markers, and chests, and since patch 0.5.3 the Remnant count scales with Waystone tier, maxing out at Tier 15+. Ocean Biome locations are clearly marked on the Atlas and feature one or two exclusive Expedition modifiers by default, including additional Runic Monsters, duplicated Runic packs, and extra buried Remnants. Verisium Sentries inside Grand Expeditions further increase encounter difficulty while improving overall loot quality. The 0.5.3 reward overhaul removed basic weapon and armour chests in favor of currency, unique items, and Waystones, making Grand Expedition one of the most rewarding endgame farming activities. [(mmoso.com)](https://www.mmoso.com/news/poe-2-patch-0-5-expedition-overhaul-budget-builds)

**Faction Leaders** — Olroth, Medved, Vorana, and Uhtred — can be found and fought across the islands you discover during ocean exploration. Each is tied to their own reward table, and defeating them is part of the Expedition questline that unlocks the mechanic's pinnacle content. Working through the questline unlocks a chain of Kalguuran bosses, and those bosses drop powerful support gems and rare alloys worth significant currency on the trade market. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/100-expedition-guide-you-need-in-poe2-0-5)

**Liquid Verisium**, introduced in patch 0.5.4, is a new currency sold by Farrow in exchange for raw Verisium. Consuming it instantly triggers a random Runic Remnant encounter with a fresh set of inscriptions, eliminating the need to repeatedly farm maps for desirable runes. Results are completely random with no preview window. [(mmoso.com)](https://www.mmoso.com/news/poe-2-patch-0-5-expedition-overhaul-budget-builds)

## Strategy & Tips

- **Chain your explosives for density, not distance.** Since 0.5.3 reduced the explosive count from 20 to 15, you cannot cover the entire field. Concentrate on the clusters of high-value Remnants and Runic Monster markers rather than spreading thin. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-grand-expedition-farming-guide)
- **Detonate Remnants before Runic Monsters.** A Remnant's bonuses only apply to monsters that spawn after it explodes. Lay your fuse so that reward Remnants go off first, then the blast rolls into monster markers — this way the increased-artifact and increased-currency modifiers land on the packs you are about to fight.
- **Prioritize Remnants that boost artifacts, currency, and pack size.** Skip Remnants that add brutal monster damage if your build cannot handle the extra waves. Click an un-detonated Remnant to preview and pick its outcome before committing.
- **Use the right Master for the right situation.** For Grand Expedition maps, Jado is recommended because his mastery gives Verisium Remnants a 10% chance to re-roll on resolution, effectively giving you a second draw at the reward pool. For normal maps with standard Expedition, switch to Hilda, who increases the number of Lair Monsters and gives them more modifiers, raising their Effectiveness and improving your odds of dropping Logbooks. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/expedition-poe2-0-5-return-of-the-ancients)
- **You do not need Tablets.** Expedition has no dedicated Atlas tree. Generic nodes that increase Rare Monster quantity, rarity, and Monster Effectiveness carry over to Runic Monsters naturally. Save your Tablet budget for other mechanics.
- **If you are not finding Logbooks naturally**, a specific boss now guarantees one on defeat — you can no longer get fully locked out of the mechanic through bad luck, as was possible before patch 0.5.3.
- **The "Feeling Lucky?" Atlas node** unlocks Liquid Verisium from Farrow, letting you reroll a Remnant's rune options instead of accepting whatever it reveals by default. This is useful if you are hunting a specific rune type. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/100-expedition-guide-you-need-in-poe2-0-5)
- **Watch out for elemental immunity on certain islands.** Some V-relic islands (for example, Frigid Bluffs) make monsters immune to chaos damage. If chaos is your main damage type, avoid those islands or swap to an alternate damage source. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/100-expedition-guide-you-need-in-poe2-0-5)

## Rewards

Expedition rewards operate on a three-tiered progression system. In the **early game**, standard map Expeditions and low-affix Logbooks drop basic Verisium and generic rares with low survival pressure — perfect for accumulating starting currency. In the **mid game**, Grand Expeditions with one or two modifiers reliably yield Liquid Verisium, extra Remnants, and more Logbooks, and can be farmed endlessly with a functional build. In the **endgame**, high-density Grand Expeditions stacked with multiple modifiers produce the best returns, including rare alloys that can spike to hundreds of Divine Orbs in value. [(mmoso.com)](https://www.mmoso.com/news/poe-2-patch-0-5-expedition-overhaul-budget-builds)

The four Expedition vendors each serve a different purpose:

- **Dannig** — artifact exchange and Logbooks. Dannig is your gateway to Grand Expeditions; you speak to him to open the Expedition map and slot your Logbook.
- **Rog** — crafts armour and shields with randomized outcomes, offering a gamble for high-tier defensive gear.
- **Tujen** — haggles for currency and accessories, giving you a shot at raw currency items like Divine Orbs in exchange for your artifacts.
- **Gwennen** — gambles for weapons and uniques, the classic "Gamba" vendor that can produce chase unique items from artifact investments. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-expedition-farming-guide)

Beyond vendor interactions, the questline bosses drop powerful support gems (one notable example is a Borana Siege support worth around 40 Divine Orbs from a Medved kill), rare alloys, and Timeless Jewels worth 10+ Divine Orbs each from the pinnacle boss Olroth. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/100-expedition-guide-you-need-in-poe2-0-5)
