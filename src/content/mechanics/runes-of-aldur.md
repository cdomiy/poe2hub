## Overview

The Runes of Aldur is the first full challenge league in Path of Exile 2, introduced with the Return of the Ancients update (patch 0.5.0) on May 29, 2026. It is a league-wide mechanic that appears in every zone from the very first area of Act 1 through the entire endgame. The core interaction revolves around **Ezomyte Remnants** — ancient stone slabs carved with runic slots — which you find in each area and use to craft powerful items through a system called **Runesmithing**. The league also introduces an entirely new defensive layer (**Runic Ward**), a new crafting currency (**Verisium**), over 150 distinct rune types, 23 Kalguuran Skill gems, and a deeply reworked Expedition endgame that ties directly into the league's lore and pinnacle boss progression.

This is not a side mechanic you can ignore. Verisium, the league's primary resource, drops exclusively from Remnant encounters and fuels Runeforging — the system that adds Runic Ward to your armour and upgrades low-level Unique items. Skipping Remnants means skipping one of the most powerful crafting pillars in 0.5.

## How It Works

### Ezomyte Remnants and Runesmithing

Every area from Act 1 onward spawns an Ezomyte Remnant. Interact with it and you enter Runesmithing mode: you select Runic Recipes from the Remnant's available slots, each recipe determined by the runes already inscribed on the slab. Each Remnant carries between **2 and 10 slots**, with higher-slot Remnants being considerably rarer but allowing you to craft much rarer outputs. The critical trade-off is that **every additional rune you slot into the recipe adds another wave of empowered enemies**, and each wave layers a runic modifier onto the monsters, making them progressively harder. More runes means a harder fight but a better reward. A greedy 10-rune recipe on a fresh Act 1 character will overwhelm you — read the room before committing.

Once you commit your rune selection, the Remnant brands a pack of monsters with whatever runes you chose. Clearing those waves hands over the crafted reward. Defeated monsters also drop **Verisium**, the metal that fuels the rest of the league's systems. The recipes available depend on the runes already inscribed on each individual Remnant and the slot count, so different zones offer different possibilities.

### The Runebook

When you discover a working rune combination, it saves permanently to your **Runebook**. The Runebook turns early-game experimentation into a personal reference sheet — by the time you reach maps, you will know exactly which combos produce the currency, gems, or runes you want, and you can replicate them on every Remnant you find. You never re-learn a recipe once it is recorded.

### Farrow and League Progression

Farrow is the league's primary NPC, introduced in Act 1 at Clearfell. He unlocks the league's systems progressively through four campaign quests:

- **Act 1**: Verisium Runeforging becomes available, letting you add Runic Ward to armour at the Verisium Anvil.
- **Act 2**: Runic Alloys unlock — 13 currency items that overwrite an existing modifier on a rare item with a guaranteed one, similar to Perfect Essences but riskier since the replaced mod is random.
- **Act 3**: Unique Verisium Runeforging unlocks, allowing you to upgrade low-level Unique Weapons and Armours. Weapons gain higher base damage; armour gains improved defences and Runic Ward. This keeps campaign Uniques relevant deep into endgame.
- **Act 4**: Ancient Runes become available — 13 powerful runes with bonuses specific to each weapon type.

Do not skip Farrow's quests. Delaying them locks you out of the league's most powerful crafting options at exactly the time you need them most.

### Runic Ward

Runic Ward is the league's new defensive layer. It sits underneath your Life as a secondary pool — once your Life drops to 1, incoming damage starts consuming Ward instead, and you only die when Ward hits 0. It regenerates independently over time, giving you a critical buffer moment to pop a flask and reposition. You add Runic Ward to armour through Runeforging at the Verisium Anvil: armour below item level 55 gains it for free with no downside, while armour above level 55 trades some of its regular defences (armour, evasion, or energy shield) for Ward, making it a meaningful build choice.

Runic Ward also doubles as a resource. Kalguuran Skills and Support gems spend Runic Ward instead of mana, so the same pool that absorbs hits also fuels your new league gems. Stack Ward and its regeneration and you buy survivability and casting headroom simultaneously — offence and defence on one bar.

### New Rune Categories

The Runes of Aldur system adds an enormous variety of new craftable items from Remnants:

- **13 Runic Alloys**: Replace a random modifier on a rare item with a guaranteed one. Best used on items with one or two replaceable mods, not on near-perfect gear.
- **13 Ancient Runes**: Powerful weapon-type-specific bonuses, unlocked after Farrow's Act 4 quest.
- **13 Mythical Runes**: Early-game runes providing significant boosts for level 15+ characters.
- **3 Fluxes**: Transform resistances on your items from one element to another.
- **15 Meta-crafting Runes**: Advanced crafting options for fine-tuning endgame gear.
- **60+ Unique-based Runes**: Created by destroying a Unique item, carrying part of that Unique's effect into rune form — a build-enabling system with enormous theorycrafting potential.
- **15+ Runic Ward Runes**: Add or modify properties related to Runic Ward.
- **23 Kalguuran Skills**: Colorless league gems crafted from Remnants, including Triskelion Cascade, Frostflame Nova, Skyfall, Voltaic Barrier, and many more. Some depend on Runic Ward as a resource.
- **7 Kalguuran Supports**: Such as Concussive Runes, Fist of Kalguur, and Runic Infusion.

### Rune Effects on Monsters

Each rune type you slot into a Remnant influences the monsters that spawn. Some key examples:

- **Adaptive Rune**: Monsters gain stackable Adaptation buffs providing elemental damage mitigation.
- **Arcane Rune**: Monsters gain extra energy shield and trigger a stunning nova when ES is depleted.
- **Bloodletting Rune**: Monsters gain life leech, cannot be leeched from, and inflict Corrupted Blood on Hit.
- **Celestial Rune**: Monsters have a chance for Fire, Cold, or Lightning Explosions on Death.
- **Earth Rune**: Monsters can conjure Earthly Spires.
- **Sky Rune**: Monsters conjure elemental tornadoes.

Choosing the right rune combination is both a crafting decision and a combat decision — the runes that produce the best rewards may also spawn enemies your build is weakest against.

## Strategy & Tips

- **Engage every Remnant you find in the campaign**. Campaign Remnants provide early Verisium essential for first-tier Runeforging. Skipping them delays your Runic Ward access significantly.
- **Match Runeshape difficulty to your build**. Higher-difficulty combinations provide better rewards but spawn significantly harder enemies. Start with moderate selections until you understand the damage-type patterns, then scale up.
- **Prioritize Verisium early**. In your character's early progression, Verisium is more valuable than most rune currency options because it directly enables Runeforging. Once you have a functional Runic Ward setup, diversify into other reward types.
- **Don't dump Runic Alloys on near-perfect items**. They replace a random mod, which can destroy a good item. Use them on gear with one clearly weak modifier.
- **Use the Runebook to plan**. Before committing to a high-slot Remnant, check your Runebook for proven combinations rather than experimenting blind.
- **Consider Runic Ward as both offence and defence**. Kalguuran Skills that spend Ward reward builds that stack Ward and regen heavily, creating a playstyle where your defensive buffer is also your resource pool.

## Rewards

The Runes of Aldur league provides rewards at multiple tiers:

- **Verisium**: The primary league currency, dropped by Remnant monsters. Used for all Runeforging operations and tradable on the Currency Exchange.
- **Runic Alloys**: 13 types of targeted crafting currency that replace a random mod with a guaranteed one.
- **Ancient Runes**: Weapon-specific powerful runes from Act 4+.
- **Kalguuran Skills and Supports**: League-exclusive skill gems that interact with Runic Ward.
- **Unique-based Runes**: Destroy a Unique to create a rune carrying part of its effect.
- **Fluxes**: Elemental resistance transformation currency.
- **Meta-crafting Runes**: Advanced crafting tools for endgame optimization.

The league's challenge system awards the **Knight of Aldur Armour Set** at 2, 4, 6, and 8 challenges completed, plus pieces of the Runes of Aldur Totem Pole decoration for each challenge from the first onward.
