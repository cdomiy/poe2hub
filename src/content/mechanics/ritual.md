## Core loop

Kill monsters standing inside a Ritual altar's glowing circle to earn Tribute. Touching the
altar starts the ritual itself, reviving the monsters you just killed so you fight them again
for the actual rewards. Spend Tribute at the altar's reward window on Omens, unique items, or
defer rewards for later. Monsters killed at one altar respawn at every other Ritual altar in the
same map, so Tribute gains compound the more altars you're running per map.

## The Ritual ladder

- Standard Ritual: the everyday version that rolls naturally on maps, or can be guaranteed
  in a region with a Ritual Precursor Tablet slotted into a completed Tower.
- Queen's Ritual: a rare upgrade, unlocked by allocating the Mysterious Rites Atlas
  passive, which gives every Ritual an 8% chance to roll into this version instead. It ends in an
  optional fight against The Queen in the Mists, who drops one of three new Corrupted Idols
  (socketable augments with strong, build-defining stats and real downsides).
- An Audience with the King: instead of spending Tribute on rewards, you can offer it toward
  summoning the King in the Mists for a guaranteed high-value unique fight.
- Rite of the Nameless: a continuous Ritual chain that runs across five linked maps. Each
  final ritual in the chain drops a boss-key fragment, collect all five to unlock the pinnacle
  fight.
- The Bodach: the Ritual pinnacle boss, reached after completing the Rite of the Nameless.

## Practical tips

- Clear Ritual encounters before engaging map bosses where possible, dying mid-boss-fight can
  wipe the map's remaining content, including progress toward the Rite of the Nameless.
- The Ritual circle can get genuinely cramped once several bosses and packs overlap inside it,
  bring real defensive layers rather than relying purely on burst damage to clear it safely.
- If you're chasing Queen's Rituals specifically for their Omen density, stack Ritual Precursor
  Tablets to force more altars per map and increase how often the 8% roll comes up.
