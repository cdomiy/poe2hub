## Overview

The Atlas of Worlds is Path of Exile 2's endgame mapping system, and in patch 0.5.0 Return of the Ancients it received a full overhaul tied to the endgame rework. The system revolves around three interconnected mechanics: **Waystones** (the items that open maps), **Precursor Towers** (special map nodes that unlock Tablet slots and reveal Atlas regions), and **Precursor Tablets** (the tool for applying specific league mechanics to Tower regions). The Atlas now has fixed points of interest at specific locations, giving players distinct objectives that are easy to find. All league mechanics on the Atlas have quests that introduce the mechanic and guide players through its various stages up to the Pinnacle Boss. The Precursor Fortress serves as the main progression hub, and the Atlas Passive Tree has been expanded to roughly 300 nodes — enough that after completing all the maps inside the Fortress, you gain enough passive points to fully allocate the tree, eliminating the need for respecialisation. [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3932540) [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg/poe-2/guides/endgame-fortress)

## How It Works

### Waystones

Waystones are the items you use to open Atlas maps, determining a map's tier, difficulty, and reward scaling, with up to six possible modifiers. Key rules: Waystones now always drop at your current map tier or higher, and Map Bosses guarantee a Waystone one tier higher than the map you are in. The highest tier, T16, cannot drop naturally at all — it only comes from corrupting a T15 Waystone with a Vaal Orb. Each Waystone tier corresponds to a specific area level (T1 = 65, scaling up to T15 = 79, T16 = 80+), and the tier directly determines monster level and loot quality inside the map. [(poe2db.tw)](https://poe2db.tw/Waystones)

### Precursor Towers

Towers are special map nodes scattered across the Atlas. Entering one requires a Waystone, and clearing it means killing all Rare monsters to unlock the Precursor Altar at its center. How many modifiers your Waystone carries into a Tower determines how many Tablet slots you unlock: zero modifiers gives one slot, three or more modifiers gives a second, and six modifiers (a fully-rolled Waystone) unlocks all three. Completing a Tower reveals a large region of the Atlas around it, which is why chasing Towers early is one of the highest-value habits in endgame mapping — it is how you find your route, not just a bonus objective.

### Precursor Tablets

Tablets are the tool for applying a specific mechanic (Ritual, Breach, Expedition, Delirium, and more) to every map within a completed Tower's radius, rather than hoping it rolls naturally. Each Tablet carries 10 charges, consuming one per map run within range, and a Tower holds up to three Tablets at once. Effects stack: running two or three Tablets for the same mechanic across overlapping Towers compounds your farming density substantially. Mechanic-specific Tablets drop from doing that mechanic — Breach Tablets from Breach monsters, Expedition Tablets from Expedition, and Overseer Tablets (which add Strongboxes, Shrines, and Essences) from Map Bosses.

Unlocking the full four-modifier (rare) Tablet crafting requires progressing through the Fortress. In 0.5, the unlock process changed: you need to defeat the Arbiter of Ash to unlock the third Tablet modifier slot, then defeat the Arbiter of Divinity to unlock the fourth. The four-modifier tablets are dramatically more powerful than two-modifier ones, making Fortress progression essential for serious farming. [(gamersky.com)](https://wap.gamersky.com/gl/Content-2154506.html)

### The Precursor Fortress

The first Tower you complete reveals the Precursor Fortress nearby — the main hub for earning Atlas Passive Tree points and the primary path toward the game's two central pinnacle encounters, Arbiter of Ash and Arbiter of Divinity. Inside the Fortress, completing any basic Map grants one Atlas Passive Point, and completing special areas and Pinnacle Boss fights unlocks even more points. [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg/poe-2/guides/endgame-fortress)

The Fortress has several layers:

- **First Layer**: The Ancient Gateway (entry point) leads to the Burning Monolith (Arbiter of Ash arena). To access the fight, you must first collect Fragments from the Western and Eastern Gateways — special maps with powerful bosses. [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg/poe-2/guides/endgame-fortress)
- **Second Layer**: Unlocked by completing the Gateways and defeating the Arbiter of Ash. Contains the Origin Tower (Arbiter of Divinity arena), plus the Western and Eastern Enigma Chambers where two sub-bosses must be defeated to collect the Origin Cradle and Spark needed to unlock the fight. [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg/poe-2/guides/endgame-fortress)
- **Defeating the Arbiter of Divinity** opens the final sections and grants enough points to fully allocate the Atlas Tree.

Maps inside the Fortress have Ancient Modifiers that add or modify the contents of the map. Any maps with modifiers that add secondary content also implicitly add that content to the map. Over 40 Ancient Modifiers have been added that have a chance to appear on any map outside the Fortress. [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3932540)

### Atlas Passive Tree

Roughly 300 points that modify how your maps behave rather than your character directly, affecting Strongboxes, Shrines, Azmerian Spirits, Essences, Rogue Exiles, Summoning Circles, and the various league mechanic clusters (Ritual, Breach, Delirium, Abyss, Expedition, and so on). Points come almost entirely from Precursor Fortress progression. Since the entire tree can be fully allocated, there is no need for respecialisation — multi-choice nodes on the tree can be changed between options at any time. Patch 0.5.1 added an additional 24 Passive Skills to the top section, particularly affecting individual biomes, and Fortress Towers, Gateways, Enigma Chambers, and Halls now award more Atlas Passive Skill Points. [(pathofexile.com)](https://www.pathofexile.com/forum/view-thread/3949114)

## Strategy & Tips

- **Do not run every map inside the Fortress individually.** Path to your quest objectives via the shortest route, since defeating the Fortress's pinnacle boss lets you auto-complete entire unexplored sections at once.
- **Prioritize Waystone-sustain Atlas passives before specializing into a specific mechanic.** Running out of Waystones before reaching red-tier maps stalls your whole run. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/how-to-fully-optimize-your-atlas-tree-in-0-5-path-of-exile-2)
- **Pick one "money mechanic" to build your Atlas tree and Tablet stock around** rather than spreading investment thin across everything. Specialization consistently out-earns generalist setups.
- **Chase Towers early.** Completing Towers reveals large regions of the Atlas, making them the most efficient way to find your route and discover content. The Tablet slots they unlock are the primary way to force specific mechanics into your mapping rotation.
- **Stack Tablets for compounding returns.** Two or three Tablets for the same mechanic across overlapping Towers dramatically increases farming density. This is the core of high-end mapping strategies.
- **When starting out, rush the generic loot multipliers first** — increased number of rare monsters, increased magic monsters, Talisman effect, and biome bonuses that apply to every map regardless of which mechanic you run. These nodes scale your rarity, quantity and Talisman effect across the whole Atlas, making them the best baseline before you specialize. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/how-to-fully-optimize-your-atlas-tree-in-0-5-path-of-exile-2)
- **Biome choices matter for map juicing.** City = extra tablet. Desert = rare effect / increased rares. Mountain = tablet effect, shrines, strongboxes. Grass = pack size, spears, summoning circles. Forest = most increased rare monsters (up to ~65%). Swamp = best boss-juicing biome. Water = weakest, mostly magic-mob pack size. [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com/guides/how-to-fully-optimize-your-atlas-tree-in-0-5-path-of-exile-2)
- **See the [Masters of the Atlas guide](/mechanics/atlas-masters/) for how Jado, Doryani, and Hilda layer on top of this system** — they modify individual maps rather than whole Tower regions.

## Rewards

The Atlas system's rewards are inherently tied to the mechanics it enables, but several direct rewards come from Fortress progression:

- **Atlas Passive Points** — the primary reward from Fortress maps and bosses. Fully completing the Fortress grants enough points to fully allocate the ~300-node tree.
- **Tablet Modifier Unlocks** — defeating the Arbiter of Ash unlocks the third Tablet modifier; defeating the Arbiter of Divinity unlocks the fourth, enabling much more powerful Tablets.
- **Ancient Modifiers** — over 40 map modifiers that can appear on any map outside the Fortress, adding variety and potential value to every map run.
- **Pinnacle Boss Drops** — both the Arbiter of Ash and Arbiter of Divinity drop high-value unique items and progression materials.
- **Auto-completed Regions** — after defeating a pinnacle boss, entire unexplored sections can be auto-completed, saving enormous amounts of mapping time.
