## Overview

The Passive Skill Tree in Path of Exile 2 is the foundational character-progression system that defines how every build functions. Unlike most RPGs where skill trees offer incremental stat boosts, PoE2's tree contains nodes that can completely rewrite how your character plays — particularly the Keystone passives, which trade powerful bonuses for equally powerful drawbacks. The tree is divided into three main attribute sections: Strength (red), Dexterity (green), and Intelligence (blue), with each class starting in a different position and having natural access to certain keystones and notables. Every class can access every part of the tree, but travelling far from your starting zone costs more passive points. Patch 0.5 added 19 new Companion-themed passive skills and reworked several major keystones, making the tree more build-diverse than ever. Understanding the tree's structure and how to path efficiently through it is the single most important skill for building a successful character in PoE2. [(exitlag.com)](https://www.exitlag.com/blog/path-of-exile-2-builds-guide/) [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)

## How It Works

**The Four Node Types** form the tree's hierarchy, each a step up in power and scarcity:

- **Small Passives** — minor stat bonuses (a few points of life, some elemental resistance, a percentage of increased damage). They exist primarily to connect you to something bigger.
- **Notable Passives** — the medium-sized, named nodes that most build planning actually revolves around. They gate a meaningful bonus (like "20% increased elemental damage with spells" or "+15 to maximum life") behind a few small passives. Notables form the backbone of every build's scaling strategy.
- **Jewel Sockets** — think of these as customizable Notables. Socketing a jewel is free and reversible at any time, and some unique jewels transform every passive within a radius rather than just adding stats. After the 0.2.0 patch removed 12 Jewel Sockets, the remaining ones are more valuable than ever and require more deliberate pathing to reach.
- **Keystones** — the biggest, rarest nodes, and the ones that actually rewrite how your character plays rather than just adding a number. Most builds anchor around exactly one Keystone. Taking a Keystone is a build-defining decision that commits you to a specific playstyle.

**Attribute Highways** — Three rings of attribute nodes circle the tree at increasing distance from center, forming what the community calls "attribute highways." They double as efficient travel paths between distant parts of the tree as well as your source of Strength, Dexterity, and Intelligence. Each class starts in the attribute quadrant matching its archetype, and the starting nodes around it are almost always relevant no matter what you end up building.

**Respeccing** — Removing an allocated node costs Gold, and the cost rises as your character levels. Talk to The Hooded One (or Doryani) in town to do it. You do not have to refund your entire tree to adjust a few nodes; individual passives can be refunded one at a time. Note that jewels need to be removed from their sockets first if you want to mass-refund everything at once.

**Key Reworked Keystones in 0.4.0/0.5.0** — Several keystones have been significantly reworked:

- **Ancestral Bond** — now causes placing totems to no longer consume resources or charges. Totems still cost 75 Spirit each, and the Keystone now doubles your totem count limit (previously unlimited). [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)
- **Trusted Kinship** — reworked: companion skill reservation effectiveness is increased by 30%, while non-companion skill reservation effectiveness is decreased by 20%. A powerful choice for minion/companion builds but a strict downside for everyone else. [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)
- **Vaal Pact** — completely reworked. It no longer provides "life leech is instant" and "cannot use life flasks." Instead: life leech amount is increased by 50%, life leech speed is reduced by 67%, you cannot recover life by any means other than leeching, and life leech effects are not removed when unreserved life is filled. This makes Vaal Pact a sustained-leech archetype rather than an instant-leech one. [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)

**Other Notable Changes** — Resilient Recovery has been replaced by Ironclad Aegis (doubles Armour, Evasion, and Energy Shield from equipped shield). Arcane Elixir now grants 10% increased cast speed if you have used a mana flask recently (previously 25% increased energy shield recharge rate). Forest Barrier now grants 10% faster energy shield recharge startup (previously 15%). Linked Casting now grants 6% increased cast speed per different spell cast in the past 8 seconds (previously 4 seconds). Breathing Chakra now grants 20% faster energy shield recharge startup. [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)

## Strategy & Tips

- **Pick your target Keystone before you start allocating anything.** You likely will not reach it until partway through the campaign, but it determines your whole routing plan. If you want Eldritch Battery, you need to path toward the Intelligence quadrant from the start, even if your class begins on the opposite side.
- **Always path toward the next Notable rather than detouring for individual small passives.** A two-node detour for a minor bonus is a point that could have moved you closer to a cluster that actually anchors your build. [(exitlag.com)](https://www.exitlag.com/blog/path-of-exile-2-builds-guide/)
- **The first 10-15 nodes you allocate typically account for the large majority of your passive tree's total power contribution.** The rest is optimization, not necessity. Do not stress about perfect pathing early — get to your first Notable cluster and your Keystone, and refine from there.
- **Jewel Sockets are worth prioritizing early, but only once you actually have a jewel worth putting in them.** Rushing an empty socket at level 15 usually is not worth the detour. Wait until you have a strong jewel, then path to the nearest socket.
- **Instilling Notables from Delirium does not double-dip.** If you have already allocated a Notable on your tree, instilling the same one onto an amulet via Liquid Emotions does not grant the effect twice. Pick notables you cannot easily path to. [(timesaver.gg)](https://timesaver.gg/blog/poe2-delirium-farming-guide)
- **Vaal Pact is now a sustained-leech choice, not instant leech.** If you are coming from PoE1 or pre-0.4 builds expecting instant leech, Vaal Pact no longer works that way. Plan your defenses accordingly — you cannot use life flasks and leech is slower, but leech continues even at full life.
- **Ancestral Bond's totem limit is now doubled, not unlimited.** If you are building a totem character, you need to factor in the Spirit cost (75 per totem) and the doubled cap rather than assuming infinite totems. [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)
- **Trusted Kinship is a trap for non-companion builds.** The 20% decreased reservation effectiveness for non-companion skills is a severe penalty that will cripple most non-minion characters. Only take this Keystone if you are fully committed to a companion-focused build.

## Rewards

The Passive Skill Tree itself is not a "reward" system in the traditional sense, but it enables and amplifies every other reward system in the game:

- **Keystones** provide build-defining mechanics that unlock entirely new playstyles (totem builds with Ancestral Bond, sustained-leech builds with Vaal Pact, companion builds with Trusted Kinship).
- **Notable Passives** are the primary way to scale your character's damage, defenses, and utility. Efficient Notable selection is the difference between a build that clears red maps and one that stalls in yellows.
- **Jewel Sockets** offer the most flexible customization, allowing you to tailor your tree to your exact needs with jewels that can add stats, transform nearby passives, or provide unique conditional bonuses.
- **Companion-themed Passives** (added in 0.5) open up new minion and companion build archetypes that were not viable before, expanding the tree's build diversity significantly. [(3dmgame.com)](https://m.3dmgame.com/ol/gl/341645.html)
