## Class Overview

The Ranger is Path of Exile 2's pure Dexterity ranged class, built entirely around bow combat and projectile scaling. If the Monk is the game's execution-heavy melee class, the Ranger is its execution-heavy ranged equivalent — a class that rewards positioning, kiting, and projectile manipulation with some of the fastest clear speeds in the game. The Ranger's identity is unambiguous: you stand far away and shoot things until they stop moving.

What makes the Ranger compelling in PoE2's current meta is how dramatically its two Ascendancies diverge in playstyle. Deadeye is a pure projectile-scaling powerhouse that turns every arrow into a screen-clearing tool, while Pathfinder trades that raw speed for a poison-stacking damage-over-time approach that excels in sustained encounters. This split gives the Ranger genuine build diversity without sacrificing class identity — you're always a bow user, but how that bow kills changes substantially between Ascendancies [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

The class's Dexterity-only attribute focus means its passive tree is tightly concentrated around evasion, projectile damage, and movement speed. This creates a natural glass-cannon profile: you clear fast, you move fast, but when something hits you, it hurts. Managing that fragility through positioning and kiting is the core skill expression of the class.

## Core Mechanics

**Projectile Scaling.** The Ranger's entire damage model revolves around projectile modifiers: additional arrows, chain, pierce, and fork. These modifiers interact multiplicatively with skill gems, creating situations where a single skill like Lightning Arrow can hit a screen of enemies multiple times per cast. Understanding how projectile behaviors stack and interact is the single most important mechanical knowledge for Ranger players [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

**Bow Bases and Weapon Progression.** Bows in PoE2 have base types that range from fast, low-damage short bows to slow, hard-hitting siege bows. The Obliterator Bow base is particularly notable for endgame Deadeye builds due to its high physical damage ceiling, though it requires significant attribute investment to equip [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com). During leveling, any bow with attack speed and added elemental damage performs well.

**Mobility and Kiting.** The Ranger's defensive model is fundamentally about not getting hit. Evasion provides a chance to avoid attacks entirely, and the class's natural movement speed advantage means you can maintain distance from most threats. This creates a combat rhythm of shoot-move-shoot that's intuitive for players coming from other ARPG ranged classes but demands constant attention — standing still is the fastest way to die.

**Spear Synergy (Cross-Class).** Although spears are technically associated with the Huntress, the Twister and Whirling Slash spear skills are commonly used by Rangers in the current meta. This cross-class skill access gives Rangers an alternative melee-range option for tight corridors where bow range is a liability [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

## Ascendancy Comparison

| Ascendancy | Tier | League Start | Endgame | Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deadeye | S | Excellent | Excellent | Projectile scaling, screen-wide clear |
| Pathfinder | B | Good (expensive) | Niche | Poison DoT, sustained single-target |

**Deadeye** holds an S-Tier rating across virtually every community tier list, and for good reason. Its projectile-scaling notables create an exponential damage curve where each additional arrow is worth more than the last due to chain and pierce interactions. Lightning Arrow combined with Lightning Rod is the current meta build, creating an automated area-denial system where Lightning Rod procs trigger Lightning Arrow chains that in turn trigger more Lightning Rods — a cascading loop that clears entire screens with minimal player input [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg). Key notables include Gathering Winds (tailwind effect on kill, stacking movement speed and damage) and Mirage Deadeye (creates a mirage that duplicates your attacks, effectively doubling your output).

**Pathfinder** sits at B-Tier for league starting, a significant step down from Deadeye. Its poison-focused identity has been weakened by Patch 0.5's nerfs to poison damage scaling, and the build now splits into two very different cost tiers: expensive Pathfinder setups with specific unique items perform well, while budget versions struggle to reach the damage thresholds needed for comfortable mapping. The Ice Shot plus Kaom's Gambit variant has emerged as a compromise, blending cold damage with poison application for a more forgiving damage profile [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com) [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

## Leveling Guide

Deadeye is the recommended starting Ascendancy for virtually all Ranger players, regardless of your intended endgame build. The projectile-scaling nodes come online immediately and scale smoothly through the campaign. Prioritize Gathering Winds first (movement speed and damage on kill keeps you safe and fast), then Mirage Deadeye (doubling your attacks is the single largest damage spike in the Ascendancy).

For active skill gems, start with a standard Lightning Arrow setup and add Lightning Rod as soon as it becomes available. The interaction between these two skills defines Deadeye's clear power. For single-target encounters like campaign bosses, swap to a concentrated barrage or a pierce-focused setup that can hit the same target multiple times per cast [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

Pathfinder leveling is notably rougher. Poison scaling doesn't function well with the limited gem links available during the campaign, and the build's damage-over-time identity means enemies live longer — which is dangerous for a squishy ranged class. If you're committed to Pathfinder, consider leveling as Deadeye and respeccing after the campaign, or invest in evasion and movement speed on the tree to compensate for the slower kill times.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Deadeye offers some of the fastest clear speeds in the game
- Pure Dexterity tree is streamlined and easy to navigate
- High mobility makes the class forgiving for players with good positioning
- Lightning Arrow + Lightning Rod is one of the most automated clear builds available
- Bow weapon class has strong crafting options and base variety

**Cons:**
- Naturally fragile — evasion is unreliable against large hits and spell damage
- Pathfinder is currently underperforming after Patch 0.5 poison nerfs
- Requires constant movement and attention; cannot facetank content
- Bow-only weapon restriction limits build diversity compared to melee classes
- Late-game scaling can plateau without specific unique items

## Recommended Starter Build

**Deadeye Lightning Arrow / Lightning Rod** is the consensus best starter for Ranger. The build creates a cascading interaction between Lightning Arrow (hits multiple targets) and Lightning Rod (auto-triggers when enemies are near), resulting in a screen-wide chain reaction that requires minimal targeting. Gathering Winds keeps your movement speed stacking on kill, and Mirage Deadeye doubles the entire setup.

Gear requirements are modest for the campaign: any bow with added lightning damage and attack speed works well. The Obliterator Bow base becomes the endgame target for its high physical damage, which converts efficiently to elemental damage via skill gem support. For defenses, stack evasion rating and consider a rare chest with the Evasion-and-Energy-Shield hybrid base for added buffer against spell damage [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg) [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com).

## Patch 0.5 Notes Impact

Patch 0.5 hit Pathfinder significantly harder than Deadeye. Poison damage scaling was nerfed across the board, reducing the damage-over-time ceiling that Pathfinder relies on for both clear and single-target. The nerf was severe enough that community tier lists dropped Pathfinder from A-Tier to B-Tier for league starting [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro). Deadeye was largely unaffected by the patch, as its projectile-scaling identity doesn't depend on the affected mechanics. The Lightning Arrow + Lightning Rod interaction was not changed, preserving the build's signature power level. General monster HP adjustments in 0.5 affected all builds equally and did not shift the Ranger's relative position in the meta.

## Choosing an Ascendancy

Deadeye is the stronger, more forgiving league-starter pick right now, its projectile-scaling
nodes and mobility make for a smooth ride from leveling through mapping. Pathfinder trades some
of that clear speed for a poison-stacking damage-over-time playstyle that's genuinely efficient
against tough single targets, better suited to players who specifically want that kind of
combat rhythm rather than pure speed-clearing.
