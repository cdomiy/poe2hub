## Class Overview

The Witch is Path of Exile 2's Intelligence-based occult class, a master of profane rituals, crippling curses, and undead minion armies. Where the Sorceress channels raw elemental power, the Witch deals in darker arts — chaos damage that bypasses conventional resistances, curses that weaken enemies into submission, and skeletal minions that fight and die at your command. The class splits cleanly between two archetypes: the minion commander who lets summons do the fighting, and the direct-damage caster who wields chaos and bone spells personally.

The Witch's design philosophy is built on trade-offs. Minion builds are among the most forgiving in the game because your summons absorb hits for you, creating a natural defensive buffer. Direct-damage builds, particularly Blood Mage, trade that safety for extraordinary damage output that costs your own life to cast. This risk-reward spectrum makes the Witch one of the most strategically interesting classes in PoE2, with each Ascendancy occupying a distinct point on that spectrum [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

The Intelligence attribute focus gives the Witch access to energy shield and minion scaling on the passive tree. The tree is dense with curse effectiveness, minion damage, and chaos damage nodes, creating efficient pathing for any of the three Ascendancies. This density means the Witch can specialize deeply without sacrificing core functionality — a well-built Witch is rarely short on either damage or survivability, though balancing the two requires intentional investment.

## Core Mechanics

**Minion Command.** The Witch's most iconic mechanic is minion summoning. Undead minions — skeletons, zombies, and spectres — form the backbone of the Infernalist's army. Minions scale with the player's passive tree investment and gem level rather than gear, making minion builds inherently gear-independent for campaign progression. The key strategic decision is how much passive tree investment to allocate to minion survival versus minion damage; a dead minion deals zero damage, so early investment in minion life and resistances pays dividends [(aoeah.com)](https://aoeah.com).

**Chaos Damage.** Chaos damage is the Witch's elemental specialty, and it has a unique mechanical advantage: chaos damage bypasses energy shield on enemies, and many monsters have lower chaos resistance than elemental resistances. This makes chaos-focused builds like Lich and certain Blood Mage variants naturally efficient against a wide range of content [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

**Curse Stacking.** The Witch is the premier curse class in PoE2, with access to curse effectiveness nodes and Ascendancy-specific curse interactions. Curses reduce enemy damage, resistances, and speed, providing both offensive and defensive value. The Lich Ascendancy pushes curse stacking to its extreme with notables that ignore curse limits, enabling layer upon layer of debuff on a single target.

**Life-Cost Casting (Blood Mage).** Blood Mage replaces mana with life as the casting resource, creating a fundamentally different resource management challenge. Every spell costs hit points, which means damage output is directly limited by your life pool and regeneration rate. This creates a playstyle where you're constantly dancing between dealing massive damage and killing yourself — the highest risk-reward dynamic in the class [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

## Ascendancy Comparison

| Ascendancy | Tier | League Start | Endgame | Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Infernalist | S | Excellent | Excellent | Fire/minion hybrid, Pyromantic Pact loop |
| Lich | A | Good | Excellent | Curse/chaos, ED+Contagion push |
| Blood Mage | C | Weak | Niche | Life-cost casting, high ceiling |

**Infernalist** is the Witch's S-Tier Ascendancy and the consensus recommended starting point. Its power comes from the Pyromantic Pact loop — a self-sustaining cycle where fire spells generate resources that fuel more fire spells and empower minions simultaneously. Demon Form stacks additional damage multipliers, creating exponential scaling as the fight continues. The Sacrosanctum unique item is a key endgame pickup for the build. Community reports indicate that Infernalist can clear endgame content on a budget of approximately five Divine Orbs, making it one of the most cost-effective top-tier builds in the game [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg) [(aoeah.com)](https://aoeah.com).

**Lich** occupies A-Tier as a strong endgame option with real group-play value. The ED+Contagion combination is the core clear strategy: Contagion spreads chaos damage between enemies, and Essence Drain provides the single-target damage that Contagion lacks. The Abyssal Plants variant offers an alternative build path. Patch 0.5.1 fixed the Eternal Life notable interaction that was causing issues with the build's sustain loop. Lich's Unholy Might buff provides group utility that makes it valuable in party play [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg) [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

**Blood Mage** is the highest-ceiling but lowest-floor Ascendancy for the Witch. Life-cost casting means your damage output is theoretically unlimited — you can cast as fast as you can spend life — but the practical constraint is staying alive while doing so. Scattering Calamity and the Unborn Lich staff enable specific interactions that push the build's damage to extreme levels, and Vitality Siphon provides the sustain needed to survive the self-damage. Blood Mage is currently rated C-Tier as a starter because the life-cost mechanic is punishing during the campaign when your life pool and regeneration are low [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

## Leveling Guide

Infernalist is the clear recommended leveling Ascendancy for the Witch, and this recommendation is nearly unanimous across community guides. The fire-and-minion hybrid comes online during the campaign and remains effective without specific gear. Start by investing in minion life and damage on the passive tree, then transition into fire spell scaling as the Pyromantic Pact loop becomes available. The leveling experience is forgiving because your minions absorb most incoming damage, giving you time to position and cast from safety [(aoeah.com)](https://aoeah.com).

Lich leveling is viable but requires more active play. Essence Drain and Contagion are available relatively early, but their damage doesn't scale efficiently without curse investment and specific gem links. Prioritize curse effectiveness on the passive tree and look for gear with chaos damage and energy shield. The ED+Contagion combo excels in dense pack situations but struggles against isolated bosses during the campaign [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

Blood Mage leveling is the most challenging of the three. Life-cost casting during the campaign means you're constantly at risk of killing yourself, and the sustain tools (Vitality Siphon, life regeneration) don't provide enough recovery at low levels to offset the life expenditure. If you're set on Blood Mage, consider leveling as Infernalist and respeccing after the campaign when your life pool and regeneration are sufficient to sustain the life-cost mechanic.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Infernalist is one of the strongest and most cost-effective league starters in the game
- Minion builds provide a natural defensive buffer that's forgiving for new players
- Chaos damage bypasses energy shield and often faces lower enemy resistance
- Lich provides genuine group-play value through Unholy Might
- Three Ascendancies offer dramatically different playstyles

**Cons:**
- Blood Mage is genuinely difficult to pilot safely, especially during the campaign
- Minion AI can be unreliable, particularly against moving targets
- Chaos damage is less effective against chaos-resistant endgame bosses
- The class's Intelligence focus means limited access to armour and evasion without specific tree pathing
- Infernalist's dominance can make the other two Ascendancies feel underwhelming by comparison

## Recommended Starter Build

**Infernalist Fire/Minion Hybrid** is the consensus best starter for the Witch and one of the best starters in the entire game. The build uses fire spells to trigger Pyromantic Pact, which in turn empowers your minion army. Demon Form stacks damage multipliers over time, creating a build that gets stronger the longer the fight goes. The core loop is straightforward: summon minions, cast fire spells to trigger Pact, let Demon Form stack, and watch everything die.

Gear requirements are remarkably low for the campaign. Any wand or sceptre with spell damage works, and minion survival scales primarily from passive tree investment. The five-Divine-Orb endgame budget reported by community sources makes this one of the most accessible top-tier builds available [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg) [(aoeah.com)](https://aoeah.com). The Sacrosanctum unique is the primary endgame upgrade target, providing a significant power spike for mapping and boss encounters.

## Patch 0.5 Notes Impact

Patch 0.5's impact on the Witch class was mixed. The general ES nerf affected Lich more than Infernalist (which relies on minions for defense) or Blood Mage (which uses life, not ES). Poison damage nerfs indirectly affected certain Witch build variants that use poison as a supplementary damage layer, though the class's core chaos and fire identities were not directly targeted [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

Patch 0.5.1 was more positive for the Witch, particularly for Lich. The Eternal Life notable fix resolved a sustain interaction that was causing the build to underperform, and the Attunement support gem benefits several Witch spell setups. Blood Mage received no targeted changes in either patch, leaving it in the same C-Tier starter position [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg). Infernalist remained the strongest Witch Ascendancy throughout both patches, largely unaffected by the balance changes and benefiting from the relative nerfs to competing builds.
