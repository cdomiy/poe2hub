## Class Overview

The Monk is Path of Exile 2's Dexterity-Intelligence hybrid melee class, built around a philosophy of speed, precision, and momentum. Where the Warrior plants his feet and swings, the Monk dashes between targets, stacking combo counters and detonating charged bells for devastating burst damage. The class's signature weapon, the quarterstaff, supports a fluid combat loop of gap-closing strikes, evasive repositioning, and elemental spellweaving that makes it one of the most visually satisfying classes to play in the current meta.

Monk occupies a unique design space in PoE2: it is simultaneously the game's most execution-heavy melee class and one of its strongest league starters. This duality comes down to the Martial Artist Ascendancy, whose bell mechanics function at full power with essentially zero gear investment — a rarity in a game where most top-tier builds require specific uniques or crafted rare bases to come online. The class rewards mechanical skill, and players who enjoy rhythm-game-style combo execution will find the Monk deeply satisfying [(rpgstash.com)](https://rpgstash.com/blog/poe2-martial-artist-guide).

The Dexterity-Intelligence attribute split gives Monk access to both evasion and energy shield scaling on the passive tree, creating natural defensive layers that complement its hit-and-run playstyle. This also opens up hybrid scaling paths — such as converting evasion rating to attack speed via the Hollow Palm Technique notable — that are unique to the class [(skycoach.gg)](https://skycoach.gg).

## Core Mechanics

**Combo and Momentum.** The Monk's combat revolves around building and spending combo resources. Melee strikes with the quarterstaff generate combo points, and certain skills consume those points for amplified effects. The pacing feels similar to fighting-game combo strings: light attacks build resources, heavy finishers spend them. This creates a natural rhythm where the Monk is weakest at the start of an engagement and reaches peak damage output after a few seconds of sustained contact [(vietrpg.com)](https://vietrpg.com).

**Bell Mechanic (Martial Artist).** Martial Artist's defining feature is the bell system. The Hollow Focus notable lets you place a resonating bell that stores damage; when the bell is struck again, it releases all stored damage as a massive burst. Because the bell's damage scales with your combo counter rather than your weapon, the entire loop functions without requiring a specific quarterstaff or unique item — a genuine rarity for a top-tier build [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

**Hollow Palm Technique.** This keystone-style notable converts evasion rating into attack speed and energy shield into critical strike chance. For builds that stack evasion and ES naturally (which the Monk's tree encourages), this provides a massive amount of offensive scaling "for free" from defensive investments. The interaction is central to why Martial Artist scales so efficiently with minimal gear [(genshinlab.com)](https://genshinlab.com).

**Elemental Integration.** Depending on Ascendancy, the Monk weaves elemental spells into its melee rotation. Invoker gains elemental crit scaling and cold/lightning synergies, while Acolyte of Chayula replaces the elemental identity entirely with chaos damage and the Darkness resource. This flexibility means the Monk can be built as a pure physical combo fighter or a spell-melee hybrid.

## Ascendancy Comparison

| Ascendancy | Tier | League Start | Endgame | Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Martial Artist | S | Excellent | Excellent | Bell-based burst, gear-independent |
| Invoker | S | Good | Excellent | Elemental crit, shield-and-evasion tank |
| Acolyte of Chayula | C | Weak | Niche | Chaos/Darkness, Breach synergy |

**Martial Artist** is currently rated S-Tier across multiple community tier lists for its bell mechanic that requires no gear investment to function at full power. Key notables include Hollow Focus (guaranteed-crit bell strikes), Hollow Resonance (back-bell detonation for double burst windows), and Hollow Form (phantom clones that replicate your attacks). The Way of Stonefist notable converts glove slot to Fists of Stone, enabling unarmed scaling paths, while Way of Mountain provides 15% more damage and 40% reduced minor damage taken — a rare offensive-defensive hybrid node [(rpgstash.com)](https://rpgstash.com/blog/poe2-martial-artist-guide) [(skycoach.gg)](https://skycoach.gg).

**Invoker** shares the S-Tier rating but in a "consistent" rather than "burst" category. Faith is a Choice doubles your energy shield, and Elemental Expression scales elemental damage with crit investment. The defensive model of stacking evasion and ES simultaneously makes Invoker one of the tankiest Monk builds, though it requires more passive tree investment to come online compared to Martial Artist [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

**Acolyte of Chayula** replaces the Monk's elemental identity with a Darkness resource system and chaos damage focus. Currently rated C-Tier, it struggles in the early game where Darkness generation is inconsistent, but its Breach-synergy nodes make it appealing for players who specifically enjoy that mechanic. Patch 0.5.1 fixed a bug with the Archon block interaction that was causing defensive issues [(vietrpg.com)](https://vietrpg.com).

## Leveling Guide

The Monk's leveling experience varies dramatically by Ascendancy choice. Martial Artist has the smoothest campaign run of any Monk build because its bell mechanic is available from the moment you select the Ascendancy. The recommended pathing priority is: Way of Stonefist first (enables unarmed scaling and frees the glove slot), then Way of Mountain (offensive-defensive hybrid), then Hollow Focus (bell burst), and finally Hollow Resonance (double bell windows). This order maximizes both damage and survivability during the mid-campaign stretch where most builds hit a wall [(rpgstash.com)](https://rpgstash.com/blog/poe2-martial-artist-guide).

Invoker levels well but peaks later than Martial Artist. Prioritize elemental damage nodes and energy shield on the passive tree, picking up crit scaling only after you have a stable defensive foundation. The Faith is a Choice notable should be your first Invoker node — the doubled ES provides more effective HP than any other single investment at that stage [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro).

Acolyte of Chayula has the roughest leveling experience of the three. Darkness generation is unreliable before you have sufficient skill gem links, and the chaos damage identity doesn't come online until mid-to-late campaign. If you're set on Chayula, consider starting as Martial Artist and respeccing after campaign completion.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Martial Artist is one of the few S-Tier league starters that needs zero specific gear
- Fluid, engaging combat loop that rewards mechanical skill
- Hollow Palm Technique provides free offensive scaling from defensive stats
- Multiple viable Ascendancies with distinct playstyles
- Quarterstaff offers both melee and ranged-adjacent build paths

**Cons:**
- High execution ceiling — poor play is severely punished on a squishy melee class
- Acolyte of Chayula is currently underperforming, limiting build diversity
- Combo-based damage means the class needs ramp-up time, hurting burst-window encounters
- Quarterstaff as a weapon class has fewer unique options compared to swords or bows

## Recommended Starter Build

**Martial Artist Bell Combo** is the consensus recommended starter for Monk. The core loop is simple: generate combo with quarterstaff strikes, place a bell via Hollow Focus, strike the bell to detonate stored damage. Way of Mountain keeps you alive through the 40% reduced minor damage taken, and Hollow Palm Technique converts your naturally-stacked evasion and ES into attack speed and crit respectively.

Gear priority is minimal — any quarterstaff with attack speed and physical damage works for the campaign. The only "required" item is a rare chest with evasion and energy shield rolls, which are common drops. This build typically clears the campaign in 8–10 hours for experienced players and transitions smoothly into early mapping with zero respec cost [(rpgstash.com)](https://rpgstash.com/blog/poe2-martial-artist-guide) [(skycoach.gg)](https://skycoach.gg).

## Patch 0.5 Notes Impact

Patch 0.5 brought several changes affecting Monk: energy shield values across the board were reduced, which hit Invoker's doubled-ES defensive model harder than other builds. The general ES nerf was partially offset by the Hollow Palm Technique interaction, which remained untouched. Acolyte of Chayula received a fix in Patch 0.5.1 for the Archon block interaction bug that was causing the build to take more damage than intended from blocked hits. Martial Artist was largely unaffected by the patch changes, which contributed to its rise as the dominant Monk Ascendancy [(nexttier.pro)](https://nexttier.pro) [(vietrpg.com)](https://vietrpg.com).

## Choosing an Ascendancy

Martial Artist, the newest of the three, is currently rated the strongest specifically because
its Hollow Focus bell mechanic doesn't need gear investment to function, making it an excellent
league starter. Invoker is a close second, offering a genuinely tanky shield-and-evasion
defensive model alongside elemental crit scaling. Acolyte of Chayula rounds out the class with a
chaos-and-Darkness-resource identity that's currently the weakest-rated of the three, better
suited to players specifically chasing its Breach synergy than as a first pick.
