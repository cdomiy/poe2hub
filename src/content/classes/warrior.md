## Class Overview

The Warrior is Path of Exile 2's Strength-based bruiser, a class built around the simple and satisfying fantasy of hitting things very hard with very large weapons. Where other classes dodge, weave, and manage complex resource systems, the Warrior plants its feet and swings a mace the size of a small tree — and the game rewards that directness with the highest natural survivability of any class. If the Monk is PoE2's execution ceiling, the Warrior is its execution floor: the most forgiving, least mechanically demanding class in the game.

That's not to say the Warrior lacks depth. The class's three Ascendancies offer genuinely different playstyles: Titan is a pure defensive juggernaut with the highest HP ceiling in the game, Warbringer turns the Warrior into a totem-summoning war cry specialist, and Smith of Kitava introduces a unique armour-forging mechanic that trades complexity for raw survivability. The Warrior's design philosophy is that simplicity and power aren't mutually exclusive — a principle that makes it the most recommended class for new players [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

The Strength-only attribute focus means the Warrior's passive tree is concentrated around life, armour, and physical damage. Every point of Strength increases both maximum life and melee physical damage, creating a natural scaling path where defensive and offensive investments reinforce each other. This dual scaling is the Warrior's most fundamental advantage over other classes.

## Core Mechanics

**Slam Skills.** The Warrior's signature skill type is the slam — slow, telegraphed attacks that deal massive area damage and often apply secondary effects like stun or armour break. Slams trade speed for impact: each swing matters, and missing a slam against a boss feels punishing in a way that missing a single arrow on a Ranger doesn't. This creates a combat rhythm that's methodical rather than frantic, where positioning and timing matter more than reaction speed [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

**Armour and Physical Mitigation.** The Warrior's primary defensive layer is armour, which reduces physical damage taken based on the proportion of armour to incoming hit size. Against small, rapid hits, armour is extremely effective; against massive single strikes, it's less reliable. The Titan Ascendancy pushes armour values to extreme levels — reports of 47,000+ armour on well-geared Titan builds are common — making even large physical hits manageable [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com).

**War Cries.** War cries are active skills that buff the Warrior and nearby allies, often with secondary effects like stunning enemies or granting endurance charges. Warbringer's Ascendancy makes war cries central to its playstyle by removing their cooldown, enabling permanent uptime on powerful buffs [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

**Shield Wall.** Shield Wall is a defensive skill that creates a physical barrier, absorbing incoming damage. It was significantly nerfed in Patch 0.5 — both base damage and the number of hit stages were reduced — but remains a cornerstone of Titan builds, which compensate for the nerf through sheer HP and armour values [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

## Ascendancy Comparison

| Ascendancy | Tier | League Start | Endgame | Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Titan | A | Excellent | Excellent | Maximum HP/armour, Shield Wall tank |
| Warbringer | A | Excellent | Very Good | War cry spam, totem hybrid |
| Smith of Kitava | B | Good | Good | Armour forging, maximum survivability |

**Titan** is the Warrior's most popular Ascendancy and for good reason: it has the highest HP ceiling in the game. The Nebuloch notable enables critical strike scaling through Shield Wall interactions, and the sheer volume of armour (47,000+ on endgame builds) makes physical damage nearly irrelevant. Titan's weakness is elemental damage — armour doesn't mitigate it — so the build needs to compensate through elemental resistance capping and specific passive tree pathing [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg) [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com).

**Warbringer** matches Titan's A-Tier rating with a completely different playstyle. Warcaller's Bellow removes the cooldown from war cries, enabling permanent buff uptime. Anvil's Weight provides instant armour break on hit, dramatically increasing damage output against armoured targets. The Grenade Totem variant adds a semi-summoner dimension, letting the Warrior deal damage passively while maintaining war cry uptime [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

**Smith of Kitava** sits at B-Tier for league starting, primarily because its armour-forging mechanic has a steeper learning curve. The Ascendancy's strength is raw survivability: Kitavan Engraving provides sustain that other Warrior builds lack, and Avatar of Fire enables full fire damage conversion for builds that want to bypass physical damage reflection. It's the most budget-friendly tank in the game but trades clear speed for that durability [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com).

## Leveling Guide

Titan and Warbringer are both excellent league starters with smooth leveling experiences. For Titan, prioritize life and armour nodes on the passive tree, and use Shield Wall defensively from the moment it becomes available. The Shield Wall nerf in Patch 0.5 means you can't rely on it as your sole damage source during the campaign, but it remains an excellent defensive tool.

For Warbringer, the leveling path is even more straightforward. War cries provide damage and survivability with zero gear requirements, and the Grenade Totem variant gives you a passive damage source that clears packs while you focus on positioning. Anvil's Weight should be your first notable pickup — instant armour break is a massive damage multiplier against campaign bosses [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

Smith of Kitava leveling is viable but slower. The armour-forging mechanic requires understanding which base types to craft and when, adding a layer of decision-making that Titan and Warbringer don't demand. If you're new to the game, start with Titan or Warbringer and consider Smith of Kitava for your second character.

For all Warrior builds, the campaign weapon progression is simple: use the highest physical DPS mace you can find. Added elemental damage on weapons is nice but secondary to raw physical damage, which scales with Strength.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Highest natural survivability of any class through Strength dual scaling
- Titan has the highest HP ceiling in the game
- Forgiving gameplay that punishes mistakes less severely
- Multiple viable Ascendancies with distinct playstyles
- Minimal gear requirements for effective campaign completion

**Cons:**
- Slow, telegraphed attacks require good positioning
- Armour doesn't mitigate elemental or chaos damage
- Shield Wall was significantly nerfed in Patch 0.5
- Lower clear speed than ranged or caster classes
- Smith of Kitava's forging mechanic has a steep learning curve for new players

## Recommended Starter Build

**Titan Shield Wall Tank** is the most recommended starter build for Warrior and arguably for the entire game. The build stacks life and armour to extreme values, uses Shield Wall as a primary defensive tool (despite the Patch 0.5 nerfs), and deals damage through slam skills that benefit from the Titan's massive HP pool. The Nebuloch notable enables crit scaling through Shield Wall, adding offensive depth to an otherwise defensive build.

Gear requirements are minimal: any high-physical-damage mace works for the campaign, and life/armour rare gear is among the most common drops in the game. The build's main weakness is elemental damage, so prioritize capping elemental resistances on your gear — this is the single most important defensive milestone for any Titan build [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg) [(exile.codex-wiki.com)](https://exile.codex-wiki.com).

## Patch 0.5 Notes Impact

Patch 0.5's most significant impact on the Warrior was the Shield Wall nerf: base damage was reduced and the number of hit stages was lowered, decreasing both its offensive and defensive output. The community response was mixed — Shield Wall was considered overpowered before the patch, and most players acknowledged the nerf was warranted even if the degree was debated. Titan builds compensated through their massive HP and armour pools, which were untouched by the patch [(boostmatch.gg)](https://boostmatch.gg).

Warbringer was largely unaffected by Patch 0.5, as war cries and totems weren't targeted by the balance changes. Smith of Kitava received minor quality-of-life improvements to the forging interface but no numerical changes to its key notables. Overall, the Warrior class remained in a strong position after Patch 0.5, maintaining its A-Tier league-start rating across community tier lists [(mobalytics.gg)](https://mobalytics.gg).

## Choosing an Ascendancy

Titan and Warbringer are both strong, proven league-starter picks, Titan leans purely defensive
with the highest HP ceiling in the game, while Warbringer adds totems and war cries for a more
group-friendly, semi-summoner playstyle. Smith of Kitava trades some offense for the highest raw
survivability in the class, but its custom armour-forging mechanic has a steeper learning curve
and isn't recommended as your very first Warrior build.
